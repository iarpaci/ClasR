## 1. PURPOSE (ONE SENTENCE)

To surface the implied reader profile embedded in the manuscript's
framing, language, and evidence presentation —
mapping whether the assumed reader's expertise level and expectations
are aligned with the manuscript's actual evidence threshold —
as a positioning signal, without producing evaluation.

---

## 2. DESIGN PRINCIPLE

Every manuscript implies a reader.

The implied reader is not named —
it is embedded in vocabulary choices, explanation depth,
assumption of prior knowledge, and evidence presentation style.

When the implied reader profile diverges from
the evidence threshold the manuscript actually operates at,
a positioning gap exists.

```
Implied reader signals extracted from framing and language
         →
Reader profile mapped: expert / interdisciplinary /
                        policy-adjacent / general academic
         →
Evidence threshold extracted from method and claim behavior
         →
Alignment between implied reader and evidence threshold mapped
         →
Positioning signal reported — no verdict
```

This kit does not ask whether the manuscript is well-written.
It reads whether the manuscript is positioned
for the reader it implies.

---

## 3. SCOPE — SIGNALS READ

### 3.1 Implied Reader Profile Detection

The manuscript's framing, vocabulary, and explanation depth
imply a specific type of reader.

Reader profile signals:

EXPERT:
- Field-specific terminology used without definition
- Prior literature assumed known
- Methods described at specialist level
- No conceptual scaffolding for non-specialists

INTERDISCIPLINARY:
- Key terms defined or translated across fields
- Multiple literature bases engaged
- Methods explained with non-specialist accessibility
- Cross-field assumptions made explicit

POLICY_ADJACENT:
- Findings framed in terms of implications or applications
- Technical detail reduced; outcome emphasis increased
- "Stakeholders," "practitioners," "decision-makers" referenced
- Evidence simplified for actionability

GENERAL_ACADEMIC:
- Broad framing without field-specific depth
- Conceptual rather than technical emphasis
- Findings presented narratively

Label: READER_PROFILE_[type]

---

### 3.2 Evidence Threshold Extraction

The manuscript's actual evidence threshold is read from:
- Methodological rigor (as mapped in SECTION 3)
- Claim intensity (as mapped in SECTION 4)
- Numerical precision (as mapped in SECTION 5)
- Hedging level (as mapped in SECTION 6)

Evidence threshold levels:
```
HIGH    — rigorous, multi-method, well-bounded claims
MEDIUM  — standard, single-method, conditionally bounded claims
LOW     — descriptive, exploratory, weakly bounded claims
```

---

### 3.3 Alignment Mapping

Alignment between implied reader and evidence threshold:

```
ALIGNED   — reader profile and evidence threshold are congruent
            (e.g., EXPERT reader + HIGH evidence threshold)

TENSION   — reader profile and evidence threshold diverge
            in ways that create positioning ambiguity
            (e.g., POLICY_ADJACENT framing + LOW evidence threshold —
             implications implied for an audience
             the evidence cannot fully serve)

MISMATCH  — reader profile and evidence threshold
             are structurally incompatible
             (e.g., EXPERT reader assumed, but
              methods described at introductory level;
              or GENERAL_ACADEMIC framing
              with highly specialized claims)
```

---

### 3.4 Explanation Depth Signal

The level of explanation provided for key concepts
relative to the assumed reader's prior knowledge.

Signal indicators — over-explanation:
- Basic concepts defined that the implied expert reader would know
- Foundational literature reviewed at introductory level
  in a specialist journal submission

Signal indicators — under-explanation:
- Specialist assumptions made without definition
  in a cross-disciplinary framing context
- Methods described at expert level
  when policy-adjacent framing implies broader audience

Label: EXPLANATION_DEPTH_OVER / EXPLANATION_DEPTH_UNDER / CALIBRATED

---

### 3.5 Audience Shift Signal

The manuscript's implied reader shifts across zones —
expert in methods, policy-adjacent in discussion,
general in conclusion.

Signal indicators:
- Vocabulary register changes between sections
- Technical depth inconsistent across manuscript zones
- Conclusion introduces terms or framing absent from methods

Label: AUDIENCE_SHIFT_DETECTED

---

## 4. WHAT THIS KIT DOES NOT DO

Does not:
  Evaluate whether the manuscript is appropriate for a specific journal
  Judge whether the implied reader is the correct target
  Recommend changes to framing or language register
  Assess writing quality or clarity
  Determine whether the manuscript will be understood

Reads only:
  The structural relationship between
  the implied reader embedded in the manuscript's language
  and the evidence threshold the manuscript actually operates at

---

## 5. OUTPUT BEHAVIOR

Reader model signals are reported as:

```
READER MODEL SIGNAL

Implied reader profile:   [EXPERT / INTERDISCIPLINARY /
                           POLICY_ADJACENT / GENERAL_ACADEMIC]
Evidence threshold:       [HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW]
Alignment:                [ALIGNED / TENSION / MISMATCH]
Explanation depth:        [CALIBRATED / OVER / UNDER]
Audience shift:           [DETECTED / NOT DETECTED]
```

No judgment. No recommendation.
Positioning map only.

If alignment is ALIGNED and no other signals detected:
→ Kit runs silently. No note produced.

---

## 6. Q-VARIANT CALIBRATION

Q1:
  TENSION and MISMATCH signals explicitly surfaced.
  POLICY_ADJACENT framing with LOW or MEDIUM evidence threshold
  noted as potential desk-reject signal for specialist journals.
  Q1 reviewers are most sensitive to reader-evidence misalignment.

Q2:
  All signals reported at standard detail.
  TENSION noted without escalation.

Q3:
  MISMATCH reported.
  TENSION suppressed silently unless AUDIENCE_SHIFT also detected.
  POLICY_ADJACENT framing treated as contextually appropriate.

## 8. ACTIVATION

Automatically active in SECTION 1 for every report.
Passive scan in SECTION 6.

Activates in SECTION 6 only when explanation depth
or audience shift signals are detected in language behavior.

If alignment is ALIGNED and no subsidiary signals fire:
→ Kit runs silently.

## 9. COLLISION MANAGEMENT

If READER_PROFILE_POLICY_ADJACENT overlaps with
Discussion Scope Drift Kit IMPLICATION_EXTENSION:
→ Scope Drift Kit takes precedence on structural drift behavior;
  Reader Model Kit reports positioning signal
→ Both active; reported in their respective SECTIONs

If MISMATCH overlaps with
Orientation Lens ALTERNATIVELESS_SCOPE:
→ Orientation Lens takes precedence on scope-closure behavior;
  Reader Model Kit reports reader-evidence misalignment
→ Reported in their respective SECTIONs; not merged

If AUDIENCE_SHIFT_DETECTED overlaps with
Abstract→Body Coherence Kit CLAIM_ESCALATION or CLAIM_DEFLATION:
→ Coherence Kit takes precedence on abstract→body distance;
  Reader Model Kit reports register-level shift signal
→ Both active; reported in their respective SECTIONs

If EXPLANATION_DEPTH_UNDER overlaps with
Interdisciplinary Tension Kit TENSION_SUPPRESSED:
→ Interdisciplinary Tension Kit takes precedence on
  tradition-level conflict suppression;
  Reader Model Kit reports explanation positioning signal
→ Not merged; reported in their respective SECTIONs

If READER_PROFILE detection overlaps with
Contribution Framing Kit CONTRIBUTION_SCOPE_INFLATION:
→ Contribution Kit takes precedence on novelty claim behavior;
  Reader Model Kit reports positioning alignment
→ Both may fire in SECTION 1; reported as separate sub-signals

---

## 10. REVISION ROUND INTERACTION

In Revision Round Mode, alignment and audience shift signals
carry additional weight under Q1-level sensitivity.

MISMATCH and TENSION signals in revised manuscripts
are read as residual risk — reader-evidence misalignment
is frequently identified in R1 editorial feedback
as a framing or positioning concern.

No separate flag produced.
Signal reported as normal; sensitivity applies implicitly.

---

## 11. HARD LIMITS

This kit must never:
  Evaluate whether the manuscript is appropriate for a specific journal
  Judge the writing quality or clarity of explanation
  Recommend changes to the manuscript's register or audience
  Label the manuscript as inaccessible or over-simplified
  Generate independent flags outside SECTION 1 and SECTION 6
  Act as a standalone readability or accessibility audit
