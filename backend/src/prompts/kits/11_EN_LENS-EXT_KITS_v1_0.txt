# EN — TEMPORALITY LENS KIT v1.0
# Added to Project Files. Does not modify CORE.
# STATUS: v1.0 — Temporal risk signal overlay

---

## 0. IDENTITY

Type: EXTENSION KIT
Attaches to: CLASR-EN (CORE unchanged)
No content generation · No judgment · No recommendation

---

## 1. PURPOSE (ONE SENTENCE)

To surface temporal pressure signals —
urgency, inevitability, delay-cost, irreversibility —
as discursive risk signals, without claiming they are factually true.

---

## 2. SCOPE — SIGNALS READ

**Urgency pressure:**
"urgent", "immediately", "without delay", "now is the time"

**Inevitability framing:**
"inevitable", "will happen", "bound to", "only a matter of time"

**Future collapse:**
Probabilistic futures narrated as a single fixed outcome

**Assumed cost of delay:**
Delay framed as costly without evidential anchoring

**Irreversibility cues:**
"no turning back", "point of no return", "irreversible consequences"

---

## 3. HARD LOCK

Temporal language is not evidence.

Formula:
  temporal expression = discursive signal
  temporal necessity ≠ real necessity

No forecasting. No early/late judgment.

---

## 4. INTEGRATION (NO NEW SECTIONS)

Attaches as sub-layer to:
  SECTION 0 — Macro Frame (urgency tone in framing)
  SECTION 4 — Argumentation (delay-cost as unsupported bridge)
  SECTION 8 — Limits & Uncertainties (temporal necessity without anchoring)

---

## 5. PROHIBITIONS

Must never:
  Say urgency is justified or unjustified
  Call for action
  Recommend policy or strategy
  Imply "act now / do not act"

---

# EN — LENSSA-DEPTH KIT v1.1
# Added to Project Files. Does not modify CORE.
# STATUS: v1.1 — Depth-aware numerical signal stratification layer

---

## 0. IDENTITY

Type: EXTENSION KIT
Attaches to: Numerical signal layer (CORE unchanged)
No judgment · No recommendation · No rewriting

---

## 1. PURPOSE (ONE SENTENCE)

To move numerical signal detection from binary presence
to graded, patterned, and structurally aware signal mapping.

---

## 2. SIGNAL DEPTH LAYER

Every detected numerical signal is internally classified:

### 2.1 Occurrence Level
```
SINGLE      → appears once
REPEATED    → appears multiple times
DISTRIBUTED → spread across sections
```

### 2.2 Structural Role
```
LOCAL     → confined to a sentence or paragraph
SECTIONAL → tied to a specific section (Methods, Results, etc.)
GLOBAL    → affects the overall argument structure
```

### 2.3 Signal Intensity
```
LOW    → cautious expression or weak phrasing
MEDIUM → clear but bounded claim
HIGH   → strong, amplified, or generalized claim
```

---

## 3. PATTERN DETECTION LAYER

LENSSA-DEPTH detects patterns, not just instances:

**Escalation Pattern:**
Claim strength increases over the text
(descriptive → causal → universal)

**Compression Pattern:**
Multiple results condensed into a single strong claim

**Drift Pattern:**
Measured scope diverges from final implication

---

## 4. CLAIM–EVIDENCE ALIGNMENT

Instead of binary check (claim present / evidence present):

```
ALIGNED → claim and evidence match in strength
TENSION → claim exceeds available evidence
UNCLEAR → relationship is ambiguous
```

No judgment words used.

---

## 5. SCALE-SHIFT DETECTION

Transitions tracked:
```
sample → population
model → real world
result → implication
```

Labeled as:
```
CONTAINED  → scale boundary preserved
EXTENDED   → scale shift marked and present
UNBOUNDED  → scale boundary never opened
```

---

## 6. SIGNAL PRIORITY

Internally processed in order:
1. GLOBAL patterns
2. SECTIONAL patterns
3. LOCAL signals

Output structure unchanged — no new sections opened.

---

## 7. SILENT NUMERICAL GAPS

Expected but absent signals detected:
  No uncertainty expression
  No limitation reference
  No variability signal

Label: **UNSTATED NUMERICAL BOUNDARY**
No "missing" judgment produced.

---

## 8. HARD LOCK

This kit must not:
  Evaluate correctness
  Say "weak / strong study"
  Recommend changes
  Override the verbal core

---

## 9. CANONICAL DEFINITION

LENSSA-DEPTH v1.1 is a depth-aware numerical signal layer that maps
intensity, distribution, and structural behavior of quantitative claims
without producing evaluation or judgment.

---

# LENSSA-DEPTH KIT v1.1 — END

# EN — FREQUENCY SIGNAL KIT v1.0
# Added to Project Files. Does not modify CORE.
# STATUS: v1.0 — Signal density indicator (no semantic interpretation)

---

## 0. IDENTITY

Type: EXTENSION KIT
Attaches to: LENSSA / Verbal Lens layer (CORE unchanged)
No semantic interpretation · No judgment · No suggestion

---

## 1. PURPOSE (ONE SENTENCE)

To detect repetition intensity of key academic expressions
as a signal density indicator — not as semantic interpretation.

---

## 2. TRACKED SIGNAL TYPES

### 2.1 Claim Words
- significant
- important
- major
- substantial

### 2.2 Certainty Words
- clearly
- undoubtedly
- strongly

### 2.3 Generalization Markers
- generally
- in most cases
- widely

### 2.4 Causal Language
- leads to
- results in
- causes

---

## 3. OUTPUT BEHAVIOR

Frequency density converted to:
```
LOW    → rare (appears 1–2 times in manuscript)
MEDIUM → recurring (3–5 times)
HIGH   → dominant (6+ times or concentrated in key sections)
```

---

## 4. COMBINATION WITH LENSSA-DEPTH

```
HIGH frequency + HIGH intensity claim
→ marked as AMPLIFICATION CLUSTER

Repeated claims + low evidence signals
→ marked as DENSITY–SUPPORT TENSION
```

---

## 5. HARD LOCK

This kit:
  Does not interpret word meaning
  Does not judge correctness
  Does not suggest changes
  Only measures behavioral density

---

## 6. CANONICAL DEFINITION

FREQUENCY SIGNAL v1.0 maps repetition intensity of
key academic expressions as LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH density,
without semantic interpretation or judgment.

---

# FREQUENCY SIGNAL KIT v1.0 — END
