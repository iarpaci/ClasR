---

## 1. PURPOSE (ONE SENTENCE)

To surface framing behaviors that present a single analytical
or methodological direction as the only available path —
closing off alternatives before the argument has established
why alternatives are excluded.

---

## 2. DESIGN PRINCIPLE

Orientation signals are scope-shaping behaviors.

They do not appear as explicit claims.
They operate by:
  treating one direction as inevitable
  treating one method as obvious
  treating one outcome as the only relevant one

This creates a constrained reading frame
before the evidence has justified that constraint.

---

## 3. SCOPE — SIGNALS READ

### 3.1 "Only Option" Framing

The manuscript presents its approach as the only viable one
without stating why alternatives were excluded.

Signal indicators:
- "The only way to address X is..."
- "No other method is capable of..."
- "This is the appropriate framework for..."
  (without justification for appropriateness)

---

### 3.2 Alternativeless Problem Framing

The problem is defined in a way that pre-selects the solution.

Signal indicators:
- Problem statement written so that only one method answers it
- Research question framed to exclude competing interpretations
- "Given X, it is clear that Y must be examined"
  (clarity asserted, not demonstrated)

---

### 3.3 Inevitability Language in Scope-Setting

The scope of the study is presented as naturally or logically given
rather than as a deliberate analytical choice.

Signal indicators:
- "Naturally, this study focuses on..."
- "It follows that the analysis must..."
- "The logical next step is..."

---

### 3.4 Foreclosed Comparison

The manuscript acknowledges that alternatives exist
but dismisses them without evidential basis.

Signal indicators:
- "While other approaches exist, they are not suitable here"
  (without explaining why)
- Brief citation of alternatives followed by immediate dismissal
- "Unlike X, this study..." framing used as scope justification

---

### 3.5 Threshold Lock Without Justification

A threshold, boundary, or cutoff is used
as if it were a natural or standard value
when it is in fact an analytical choice.

Signal indicators:
- Statistical significance thresholds presented as universal
- Spatial boundaries treated as given
- Temporal windows treated as obvious

---

## 4. WHAT THIS KIT DOES NOT DO

Does not:
  Evaluate whether the chosen approach is correct
  Judge whether alternatives should have been used
  Recommend methodological changes
  Label framing as biased or strategic

Reads only:
  Whether scope and aim language forecloses alternatives
  before evidence has established the basis for that foreclosure

---

## 5. OUTPUT BEHAVIOR

Orientation signals are reported as:

```
ORIENTATION SIGNAL DETECTED: [type]
Location: [SECTION 1 or argument zone]
Label: ONLY-OPTION FRAMING / ALTERNATIVELESS SCOPE /
       INEVITABILITY LANGUAGE / FORECLOSED COMPARISON /
       THRESHOLD LOCK
```

No judgment. No recommendation.
Signal + location + label only.

---

## 6. INTEGRATION — LENS-BRIDGE CONNECTION

### Primary: SECTION 1 — Aim & Scope
"Only option" framing and alternativeless language
→ reported as scope-setting sub-signal within SECTION 1

### Secondary: SECTION 4 — Argumentation
Where orientation signals enter the argument chain
as unsupported bridges or inevitability claims
→ flagged within SECTION 4 as argumentative constraint

Orientation Lens output never opens a new section.
Always appears as a sub-signal within the relevant SECTION.

---

## 7. ACTIVATION

Automatically active in SECTION 1 for every report.
Passive scan in SECTION 4.
Activates in SECTION 4 only when orientation signals
function as argumentative bridges without evidential support.

---

## 8. COLLISION MANAGEMENT

If an orientation signal overlaps with a Verbal Lens
or Temporality Lens signal:
→ reported once, in the more specifically bound SECTION
→ collision not surfaced to user

Typical collision:
Temporality Lens: "inevitability" signal → SECTION 0
Orientation Lens: "inevitability" in scope framing → SECTION 1
→ Both may fire; reported in their respective SECTIONs separately
→ Not merged unless in the same SECTION

---

## 9. HARD LOCK

This kit must never:
  Label scope choices as manipulative or misleading
  Evaluate methodological appropriateness
  Suggest alternative approaches
  Generate independent flags or scores outside SECTION 1 / SECTION 4
  Act as a standalone methodological review

---

## 10. CANONICAL DEFINITION

ORIENTATION LENS v1.0 surfaces framing behaviors that present
a single analytical direction as the only available path —
"only option" language, alternativeless scope-setting,
and threshold locks without justification —
as discursive constraint signals, without producing
judgment, evaluation, or recommendation.
