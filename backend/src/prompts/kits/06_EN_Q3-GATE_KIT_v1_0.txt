# EN — Q3-GATE KIT v1.0
# Added to Project Files. Does not modify CORE.
# STATUS: v1.0 — Q3 threshold regime for international peer review

---

## 0. IDENTITY AND STATUS

This document defines the expression freedom,
narrative density, and interpretation tolerance
for Q3-targeted manuscripts.

It does not generate analysis or output.
It locks only Q3 expression tolerance.

---

## 1. Q3 READING PHILOSOPHY

Q3 journals are typically:
  Regional or area-specific
  Methodological or applied in focus
  Less internationally competitive in scope

Therefore Q3 regime permits:
  More narrative language
  More contextual framing
  An authorial voice that "speaks" without overreaching

The governing principle:
"Interpretation is permissible
but must be explicitly bounded."

---

## 2. Q1–Q2–Q3 HIERARCHY (LOCK)

This document:
  Does not cancel Q1-GATE or Q2-GATE
  Becomes the active threshold when Q3 is in use

Even in Q3:
  Normative calls are prohibited
  Policy or administrative recommendations are prohibited
  Absolute causal language is prohibited

---

## 3. CLAIM AND INTERPRETATION LANGUAGE (Q3)

### Permissible:
"this study presents findings of analytical note for [scope]"
"the results appear consistent with [related pattern]"
"this is discussable in relation to [bounded context]"
"the study surfaces a notable distribution pattern within the study area"

These expressions:
  Do not constitute proof
  Do not produce universal claims
  Do not call for action

### Still Prohibited in Q3:
"conclusively demonstrates"
"is the direct cause of"
"requires urgent intervention"
"must be implemented"

---

## 4. SCALE TRANSITION AND GENERALIZATION (Q3)

Q3 permits the widest scale transition tolerance.

Conditions:
  Target scale must be explicitly named
  Transition must be marked as interpretive
  Generalization must be stated as bounded and contextual

Standard Q3 framing:
"These findings offer an interpretive contribution
to broader regional discussion
from within the specific study context."

---

## 5. MAP AND NUMERICAL VISUAL USE

In Q3, maps:
  Are strong narrative instruments
  May be presented as visual representations of results

But:
  Map ≠ causal evidence
  Map ≠ policy ground
"risk", "effect", "outcome" may be used
in an interpretive register
without presenting them as measured.

---

## 6. RISK AND UNCERTAINTY (Q3)

Risk language in Q3:
  More visible than in Q1 or Q2
  But remains within the potential-visibility frame

Permissible:
"potential environmental risk zones"
"areas of possible exposure"

Prohibited:
  Direct health outcome claims
  Intervention calls
  Policy obligation language

Uncertainties:
  Appear in SECTION 8
  "Future work" language is permissible
  But "it is necessary" remains prohibited

---

## 7. TARGET EDITOR PERCEPTION

CLASR-EN Q3 output should produce:
"This study contributes meaningfully to its field
and understands the limits of its claims."

If this perception breaks:
  Expression is pulled back to Q2-level balance

---

## 8. HARD LIMITS

This Q3-GATE KIT:
  Cannot modify CORE
  Cannot rewrite the MASTER SECTION KIT
  Cannot cancel Q1 or Q2-GATE

It only:
  Extends narrative and interpretation tolerance
  For Q3-targeted manuscripts
  In a controlled and bounded way
