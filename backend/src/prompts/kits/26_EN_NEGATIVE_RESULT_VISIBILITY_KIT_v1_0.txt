## 1. PURPOSE (ONE SENTENCE)

To surface how null findings, non-significant results,
and hypothesis rejections are handled within the manuscript —
mapping whether they are made visible, deflected, or absent —
as structural visibility signals, without producing evaluation.

---

## 2. DESIGN PRINCIPLE

A null result is not an absence of finding.
It is a finding with a specific visibility profile.

This kit does not ask whether null results exist in the data.
It reads how the manuscript handles results
that do not confirm the primary hypothesis or expected direction.

```
Results zone scanned for non-confirmation signals
         →
Handling behavior mapped:
  visible / deflected / absent
         →
Framing language extracted
         →
Visibility profile reported — no verdict
```

Three primary visibility states:

NULL_VISIBLE    — null or non-significant result explicitly reported
                  and integrated into the argument
NULL_DEFLECTED  — null result present but framed away
                  ("unexpected," "interesting," "warrants future study")
NULL_ABSENT     — no non-significant result visible
                  in a study design where some would be expected

---

## 3. SCOPE — SIGNALS READ

### 3.1 Explicit Null Reporting

The manuscript reports non-significant, null,
or contrary-to-hypothesis results directly.

Positive signal indicators:
- "No significant association was found between..."
- "The hypothesis that X affects Y was not supported"
- "Results were non-significant (p = ...)"
- Null result integrated into Discussion without deflection

Label: NULL_VISIBLE — INTEGRATED

---

### 3.2 Deflection Language

The manuscript acknowledges a null or unexpected result
but frames it in a way that minimizes its structural weight.

Signal indicators:
- "Interestingly, no significant effect was found..."
  (surprise framing without structural integration)
- "This unexpected finding warrants future investigation"
  (deflection to future work)
- "While X was not significant, Y demonstrated..."
  (pivot away from null without explanation)
- Non-significant results reported in supplementary materials only

Label: NULL_DEFLECTED

Deflection subtypes:
```
SURPRISE_FRAMING    — null labeled as unexpected without elaboration
FUTURE_DEFLECTION   — null redirected to future work
PIVOT_SUPPRESSION   — null acknowledged then immediately abandoned
SUPPLEMENTARY_ONLY  — null results displaced from main text
```

---

### 3.3 Hypothesis Rejection Handling

When the study tests a specific hypothesis
and the hypothesis is rejected or not supported,
how the rejection is framed is read.

Signal indicators — visible handling:
- Rejection explicitly stated
- Alternative explanation offered
- Theoretical implication of rejection discussed

Signal indicators — suppressed handling:
- Hypothesis stated in introduction
- Results section contains no clear rejection statement
- Discussion proceeds as if hypothesis was partially confirmed

Label: HYPOTHESIS_REJECTION_VISIBLE / HYPOTHESIS_REJECTION_SUPPRESSED

---

### 3.4 Structural Null Absence

In a study design where null results would be structurally expected
— comparative studies, multi-variable models, longitudinal designs —
no null or non-significant results appear anywhere in the manuscript.

This is not an accusation of selective reporting.
It is a structural visibility signal.

Signal indicators:
- Multi-variable model with all variables significant
- Comparative study where all comparisons favor primary hypothesis
- Longitudinal study with consistent directional findings
  and no contrary time points acknowledged

Label: STRUCTURAL_NULL_ABSENCE

Field-sensitivity note:
Some study designs genuinely produce uniformly significant results.
This kit reads the signal — it does not evaluate plausibility.

---

### 3.5 Selective Reporting Language

Language patterns that structurally suggest
results have been filtered before presentation.

Signal indicators:
- "Selected results are presented below..."
- "Key findings include..." (without scope of non-key findings)
- "The most relevant results are..."
- Results section organized by significance level
  with non-significant results grouped at the end
  without structural explanation

Label: SELECTIVE_PRESENTATION_SIGNAL

---

## 4. WHAT THIS KIT DOES NOT DO

Does not:
  Determine whether selective reporting actually occurred
  Evaluate the statistical validity of reported results
  Judge whether null results are meaningful or trivial
  Recommend that null results be added or expanded
  Label the manuscript as biased or incomplete
  Access or infer unpublished results

Reads only:
  How the manuscript's visible text handles
  non-confirmatory results — as behavioral framing signals

---

## 5. OUTPUT BEHAVIOR

Visibility signals are reported as:

```
NEGATIVE RESULT VISIBILITY SIGNAL

Null handling:        [NULL_VISIBLE / NULL_DEFLECTED / NULL_ABSENT]
Deflection subtype:   [if deflected: SURPRISE_FRAMING /
                       FUTURE_DEFLECTION / PIVOT_SUPPRESSION /
                       SUPPLEMENTARY_ONLY]
Hypothesis rejection: [VISIBLE / SUPPRESSED / NOT APPLICABLE]
Structural absence:   [STRUCTURAL_NULL_ABSENCE / NOT DETECTED]
Selective signal:     [SELECTIVE_PRESENTATION_SIGNAL / NOT DETECTED]
```

No judgment. No recommendation.
Visibility profile only.

---

## 6. Q-VARIANT CALIBRATION

Q1:
  NULL_DEFLECTED and STRUCTURAL_NULL_ABSENCE explicitly surfaced.
  SELECTIVE_PRESENTATION_SIGNAL noted as potential reviewer concern.
  Q1 reviewers and editors weigh null result transparency heavily.

Q2:
  All signals reported at standard detail.
  NULL_DEFLECTED noted without escalation.

Q3:
  STRUCTURAL_NULL_ABSENCE and HYPOTHESIS_REJECTION_SUPPRESSED reported.
  NULL_DEFLECTED subtypes suppressed silently unless SUPPLEMENTARY_ONLY.

---

## 7. INTEGRATION — LENS-BRIDGE CONNECTION

### Primary: SECTION 5 — Numerical / Spatial Behavior
Null result visibility and selective presentation signals
that affect how numerical outputs are represented
→ reported as visibility sub-signal within SECTION 5

### Secondary: SECTION 8 — Limits & Uncertainties
Where null result absence or deflection constitutes
an unmapped knowledge boundary —
specifically where non-confirmation signals
are redirected to "future work" rather than bounded
→ reported as limit-visibility note within SECTION 8

Negative Result Visibility Kit output never opens a new section.
Always appears as a sub-signal within the relevant SECTION.

---

## 8. ACTIVATION

Automatically active in SECTION 5 for every report
where numerical results are present.

Passive scan in SECTION 8 for deflection-to-future-work patterns.

If no numerical results are present (theoretical manuscripts):
→ Kit runs silently on SECTION 5
→ Hypothesis rejection handling still scanned in Discussion zone

---

## 9. COLLISION MANAGEMENT

If NULL_DEFLECTED overlaps with
Silence Lens SILENCED_UNCERTAINTY in SECTION 8:
→ Silence Lens takes precedence on argumentative silence;
  Negative Result Visibility Kit reports framing behavior
→ Both active; reported as separate sub-signals

If STRUCTURAL_NULL_ABSENCE overlaps with
Discussion Scope Drift Kit MECHANISM_INTRODUCTION:
→ Scope Drift Kit takes precedence on interpretive expansion;
  Negative Result Visibility Kit reports structural absence signal
→ Reported in their respective SECTIONs; not merged

If SELECTIVE_PRESENTATION_SIGNAL overlaps with
Calibration-Deep over-claim detection (§1.1):
→ Calibration-Deep governs expression correction;
  Negative Result Visibility Kit reports structural framing signal
→ Not merged; collision not surfaced to user

If HYPOTHESIS_REJECTION_SUPPRESSED overlaps with
Argument Symmetry Kit ASYMMETRIC CORE zone:
→ Both active; reported in their respective SECTIONs
→ Symmetry Kit reports zone-level ratio;
  Negative Result Visibility Kit reports hypothesis handling behavior
→ Not merged

---

## 10. REVISION ROUND INTERACTION

In Revision Round Mode, null result visibility signals
carry additional weight under Q1-level sensitivity.

NULL_DEFLECTED and STRUCTURAL_NULL_ABSENCE in revised manuscripts
are read as residual risk — transparency of non-significant results
is increasingly weighted by post-review editorial standards.

No separate flag produced.
Signal reported as normal; sensitivity applies implicitly.

---

## 11. HARD LIMITS

This kit must never:
  Infer or assert that selective reporting occurred
  Evaluate the statistical validity of results
  Recommend that specific null results be reported
  Label the manuscript as biased, incomplete, or manipulated
  Generate independent flags outside SECTION 5 and SECTION 8
  Act as a standalone research integrity audit

---

## 12. CANONICAL DEFINITION

NEGATIVE RESULT VISIBILITY KIT v1.0 surfaces how null findings,
non-significant results, and hypothesis rejections are handled
within the manuscript — mapping visibility, deflection, and absence
as structural framing signals — without producing judgment,
evaluation, or recommendation.
