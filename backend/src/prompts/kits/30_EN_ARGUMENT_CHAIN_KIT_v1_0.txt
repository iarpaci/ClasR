# STATUS: v1.0 — Longitudinal argument continuity signal detection overlay

## 0. IDENTITY

Type: EXTENSION KIT
Attaches to: CLASR-EN reading layer (CORE unchanged)
No content generation · No judgment · No recommendation

Tier: 3 — Extension
Primary SECTION: SECTION 4 — Argumentation
Secondary SECTION: SECTION 0 — Macro Frame
           SECTION 8 — Limits & Uncertainties

---

## 1. PURPOSE (ONE SENTENCE)

To surface whether the manuscript's central claim,
introduced in the framing zone, is sustained, transformed,
or lost across the full length of the argument —
mapping longitudinal continuity as a structural chain signal,
without producing judgment on the claim's validity.

---

## 2. DESIGN PRINCIPLE

The existing CLASR-EN system reads each SECTION independently.
Argument Symmetry Kit reads proportionality per zone.
Discussion Scope Drift Kit reads Results→Discussion distance.

None of these reads the full longitudinal arc:

Does the claim the manuscript opens with
arrive — intact, transformed, or broken —
at the conclusion?

```
Central claim extracted from framing zone
         →
Claim tracked across SECTION 1 → 2 → 3 → 4 → 5 → 8
         →
Continuity state mapped at each transition
         →
Chain profile assembled
         →
Drift points and recovery behavior reported
```

This is not a summary.
It is a structural continuity map of a single thread
— the manuscript's primary claim — across its full length.

---

## 3. CLAIM EXTRACTION

The central claim is extracted from the framing zone:
- Title
- Abstract claim statement
- Introduction's explicit research aim or hypothesis

If multiple claims are present:
→ The primary claim is identified as the one
  most directly tied to the stated research aim
→ Secondary claims are not tracked independently

If no explicit central claim is identifiable:
→ Label: CLAIM_NOT_ANCHORED
→ Chain tracking cannot proceed
→ Single note in SECTION 0:
  [Argument chain scan: central claim not extractable from framing zone]

---

## 4. CONTINUITY STATES

At each SECTION transition, the claim is assessed:

```
SUSTAINED    — claim present, consistent with framing zone version
NARROWED     — claim scope reduced; boundary explicitly drawn
EXPANDED     — claim scope widened beyond framing zone
TRANSFORMED  — claim modified in substance, not just scope
LOST         — claim no longer traceable in this zone
RECOVERED    — claim re-emerges after a LOST or TRANSFORMED state
```

NARROWED and RECOVERED are structurally neutral signals.
EXPANDED and TRANSFORMED are moderate signals.
LOST is a high signal.

---

## 5. TRANSITION MAP

Transitions tracked:

```
T1: Framing → Methods       (SECTION 1+2 → SECTION 3)
T2: Methods → Results       (SECTION 3 → SECTION 5)
T3: Results → Discussion    (SECTION 5 → SECTION 4)
T4: Discussion → Conclusion (SECTION 4 → SECTION 7+8)
```

Each transition receives a continuity state label.
Drift points are noted with the transition label.

---

## 6. CHAIN PROFILES

After all transitions are mapped,
a manuscript-level chain profile is assembled:

```
INTACT           — claim sustained or appropriately narrowed
                   across all transitions
PARTIALLY INTACT — one transition shows EXPANDED, TRANSFORMED,
                   or brief LOST with recovery
DRIFTED          — two or more transitions show EXPANDED
                   or TRANSFORMED without recovery
BROKEN           — LOST state at one or more transitions
                   without recovery
UNRESOLVED       — claim reaches conclusion in TRANSFORMED
                   or EXPANDED state without acknowledgment
```

---

## 7. SCOPE — ADDITIONAL SIGNALS

### 7.1 Claim Substitution

A new claim, not present in the framing zone,
emerges in the Discussion or Conclusion
and displaces the original claim.

Signal indicators:
- Conclusion centers on a finding
  not mentioned in the abstract or introduction
- "Unexpectedly, we found..." followed by
  a claim that becomes the manuscript's de facto conclusion
- The abstract's primary claim is absent from the Conclusion

Label: CLAIM_SUBSTITUTION

---

### 7.2 Claim Fragmentation

The central claim, stated as unified in the framing zone,
is operationalized as multiple disconnected sub-claims
that are never reintegrated.

Signal indicators:
- Introduction states one research question
- Results section addresses 3+ separate sub-questions
- No synthesis section reintegrates them
- Discussion treats each sub-finding independently

Label: CLAIM_FRAGMENTATION

---

### 7.3 Claim Abandonment

The central claim is introduced,
developed through Methods,
and then silently not addressed in Results or Discussion.

Signal indicators:
- Explicit hypothesis in introduction
- No results section entry for that hypothesis
- Discussion proceeds without acknowledging the gap

Label: CLAIM_ABANDONMENT

Overlap note: CLAIM_ABANDONMENT may overlap with
Negative Result Visibility Kit HYPOTHESIS_REJECTION_SUPPRESSED.
Where both fire — both reported;
Negative Result Visibility Kit takes precedence in SECTION 5,
Argument Chain Kit reports in SECTION 4.

---

## 8. OUTPUT BEHAVIOR

Reported as a sub-block within SECTION 4:

```
▸ ARGUMENT CHAIN SIGNAL

Central claim:    [extracted from framing zone — one line]
Chain profile:    [INTACT / PARTIALLY INTACT / DRIFTED /
                   BROKEN / UNRESOLVED]

Transition map:
  T1 (Framing → Methods):      [continuity state]
  T2 (Methods → Results):      [continuity state]
  T3 (Results → Discussion):   [continuity state]
  T4 (Discussion → Conclusion):[continuity state]

Additional signals: [CLAIM_SUBSTITUTION / CLAIM_FRAGMENTATION /
                     CLAIM_ABANDONMENT / none detected]
```

No judgment. No recommendation.
Chain map only.

If chain profile is INTACT and no additional signals:
→ Single line: [Argument chain: INTACT across all transitions]

---

## 9. Q-VARIANT CALIBRATION

Q1:
  DRIFTED, BROKEN, and UNRESOLVED profiles explicitly surfaced.
  CLAIM_SUBSTITUTION noted as potential reviewer concern.
  Q1 reviewers track claim continuity rigorously.

Q2:
  All profiles reported at standard detail.
  PARTIALLY INTACT noted without escalation.

Q3:
  BROKEN and UNRESOLVED profiles reported.
  DRIFTED noted at summary level.
  CLAIM_FRAGMENTATION suppressed silently
  unless also producing SCOPE_FIT_RISK (Desk-Reject Kit).

---

## 10. WHAT THIS KIT DOES NOT DO

Does not:
  Evaluate whether the central claim is correct or important
  Judge whether the claim transformation is appropriate
  Recommend how to strengthen or repair the argument chain
  Label the manuscript as logically incoherent
  Read sub-claims or secondary argument threads
  Act as a standalone argument analysis tool

Reads only:
  Whether the manuscript's single primary claim
  is structurally traceable across its full length —
  as a longitudinal continuity signal

---

## 11. INTEGRATION — LENS-BRIDGE CONNECTION

### Primary: SECTION 4 — Argumentation
Full argument chain signal and transition map
→ reported as chain sub-block within SECTION 4
→ extends (does not replace) existing argumentation signals

### Secondary: SECTION 0 — Macro Frame
Where chain profile is BROKEN or UNRESOLVED,
the macro behavioral type of the manuscript
may be misrepresented in the framing zone
→ noted as framing-chain gap within SECTION 0

### Secondary: SECTION 8 — Limits & Uncertainties
Where CLAIM_ABANDONMENT or UNRESOLVED chain profile
constitutes an unmapped knowledge boundary
→ flagged as chain-limit gap within SECTION 8

Argument Chain Kit output never opens a new section.
Always appears as a sub-block within SECTION 4
and optionally as sub-signals within SECTION 0 and SECTION 8.

---

## 12. ACTIVATION

Automatically active in SECTION 4 for every report
where framing zone and at least one body section are present.

If chain profile is INTACT and no additional signals detected:
→ Single-line output only.

If central claim is not extractable:
→ CLAIM_NOT_ANCHORED noted in SECTION 0.
→ Kit runs silently on remaining signals.

---

## 13. COLLISION MANAGEMENT

If DRIFTED or BROKEN chain overlaps with
Discussion Scope Drift Kit CAUSAL DRIFT or SCALE DRIFT:
→ Scope Drift Kit takes precedence on directional drift behavior
  in the Results→Discussion zone;
  Argument Chain Kit reports the longitudinal chain profile
→ Both active; reported in SECTION 4 as separate sub-signals

If CLAIM_SUBSTITUTION overlaps with
Abstract→Body Coherence Kit CLAIM_ESCALATION:
→ Coherence Kit takes precedence on abstract→body distance;
  Argument Chain Kit reports the intra-body substitution signal
→ Reported in their respective SECTIONs; not merged

If CLAIM_ABANDONMENT overlaps with
Negative Result Visibility Kit HYPOTHESIS_REJECTION_SUPPRESSED:
→ Both active
→ Negative Result Visibility Kit takes precedence in SECTION 5
→ Argument Chain Kit reports in SECTION 4
→ Not merged

If CLAIM_FRAGMENTATION overlaps with
Argument Symmetry Kit ASYMMETRIC CORE zone:
→ Symmetry Kit takes precedence on proportionality pattern;
  Argument Chain Kit reports structural fragmentation
→ Reported in SECTION 4 as separate sub-signals

If BROKEN or UNRESOLVED chain overlaps with
Desk-Reject Signal Kit SCOPE_FIT_RISK:
→ Both active
→ Desk-Reject Kit aggregates the chain signal into its profile;
  Argument Chain Kit reports the full transition map independently
→ Not merged

---

## 14. PARTIAL INPUT BEHAVIOR

Abstract only (Type A):
→ Central claim extractable; chain tracking not possible
→ Label: CLAIM_NOT_ANCHORED (chain not traceable — body absent)

Introduction only (Type B):
→ T1 partially assessable; T2–T4 not possible
→ Label: [Chain scan — introduction only, T1 partial]

Methods only (Type C):
→ T1 assessable if framing zone available
→ Label: [Chain scan — reduced coverage]

Results / Discussion only (Type D):
→ T3 and T4 assessable; T1 and T2 not possible
→ Partial chain map; framing zone not available for anchor
→ Label: [Chain scan — framing zone absent, partial map]

Mixed (Type E):
→ Kit runs on available zone pairs
→ Coverage noted per transition

---

## 15. REVISION ROUND INTERACTION

In Revision Round Mode, chain profile signals
carry additional weight under Q1-level sensitivity.

BROKEN and UNRESOLVED profiles in revised manuscripts
are read as residual risk — argument continuity is
among the structural concerns most frequently raised
in substantive R1 reviewer comments.

CLAIM_SUBSTITUTION in a revised manuscript is particularly weighted:
it may indicate that the revision changed the manuscript's
central claim in response to reviewers
without updating the framing zone accordingly.

No separate flag produced.
Profile reported as normal; sensitivity applies implicitly.

---

## 16. HARD LIMITS

This kit must never:
  Evaluate the validity or importance of the central claim
  Judge whether claim transformation is scientifically appropriate
  Recommend how to repair the argument chain
  Label the manuscript as logically flawed or incoherent
  Track secondary or sub-claims
  Generate independent flags outside SECTION 4, SECTION 0, SECTION 8
  Act as a standalone logical analysis or rhetoric review tool

---

## 17. CANONICAL DEFINITION

ARGUMENT CHAIN KIT v1.0 surfaces whether the manuscript's
central claim is sustained, narrowed, expanded, transformed,
or lost across its full longitudinal structure —
mapping continuity states at four argument transitions
and assembling a chain profile with additional signals
for substitution, fragmentation, and abandonment —
without producing judgment, evaluation, or recommendation.
