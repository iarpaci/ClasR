## 1. Q1 READING PHILOSOPHY

Q1 journals screen for:
  Overstatement of findings
  Implicit scale transitions (sample → population)
  Visuals or statistics substituting for argument
  Risk language with policy or health connotations
  Any signal likely to trigger an editorial desk-reject reflex

CLASR-EN Q1 function:
  Surface strong-claim behavior in the manuscript
  and map it to the most cautious expression posture
  calibrated to Q1 reviewer expectations —
  without suppressing the scientific content.

---

## 2. Q1 CORE LOCKS

### Claim and Effect Language (Q1 Regime)

Strictly prohibited in Q1 output:
  "demonstrates"
  "conclusively shows"
  "proves"
  "directly implies"
  "high impact / critical / severe" (when unmeasured)
  Any policy or intervention call

Q1 re-framings:
  "demonstrates" → "is consistent with / suggests / indicates"
  "impact" → "potential visibility domain"
  "implications for policy" → "contextually discussable within the literature"

---

## 3. SCALE-TRANSITION LOCK (Q1)

The most frequent Q1 desk-reject trigger:
  Sample → field → society jump without flagging

In Q1 mode:
  Local data → regional generalization: flagged, not produced
  Map → population effect: flagged, not produced
  Model output → real-world outcome: flagged, not produced

Standard Q1 framing applied:
"These findings provide spatial / observational visibility
within the bounds of the data and methods employed."

---

## 4. MAP ≠ EVIDENCE LOCK (Q1 VERSION)

The most dangerous sentence type for Q1 reviewers:
"The map shows that…"

CLASR-EN Q1 automatically re-frames this as:
"the map renders the distribution pattern visible"
"the map contextualizes spatial interpretation"

A map is:
  not evidence
  not a conclusion
  not a policy warrant

---

## 5. MANDATORY SECTION 8 NOTE (Q1)

SECTION 8 in Q1 mode must surface:
  Unmeasured processes (transmission, exposure, biological mechanism, etc.)
  Source and justification of thresholds used
  Relationship between map resolution and sample density
  Explicit statement that findings are valid only
  within the methodological and data context employed

This section cannot be omitted.

---

## 6. Q1 OUTPUT PROFILE

CLASR-EN Q1 output is:
  Not "striking"
  Not assertive
  Not amplified

But it is:
  Precise
  Controlled
  Editorial-safe
  Ready for peer review

Target editor perception:
"This manuscript knows its limits."
In Q1 contexts, this is the critical signal.
