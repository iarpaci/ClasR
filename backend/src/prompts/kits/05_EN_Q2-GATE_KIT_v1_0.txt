## 0. IDENTITY AND STATUS

This document defines the threshold, flexibility,
and expression tolerance regime for Q2-targeted manuscripts.

This document:
  Is not CORE
  Is not the MASTER SECTION KIT
  Does not generate analysis or output
  Locks only Q2 expression tolerance

---

## 1. Q2 READING PHILOSOPHY

Q2 journals:
  Are not as rigorous as Q1
  Are not as permissive as Q3/Q4

Therefore the Q2 regime is built on:
  Controlled flexibility
  Open but bounded interpretation
  Cautious but not entirely silent language

The governing principle:
"Claims are not fully suppressed,
but their scale is explicitly marked."

---

## 2. Q1 ↔ Q2 CORE DIFFERENCE (LOCK)

This document does not override Q1-GATE.
It becomes the active threshold when Q2 is in use.

When Q2 is active:
  Some expressions prohibited in Q1
  become permissible — conditionally and with scope marking
  Normative calls remain prohibited

---

## 3. CLAIM AND EFFECT LANGUAGE (Q2 REGIME)

### Permissible (conditional use only):
"the study indicates patterns associated with…"
"findings exhibit a distribution that is analytically notable within this context"
"results are discussable in relation to [bounded scope]"

These expressions must:
  Produce no causal claim
  Produce no generalization
  Never become policy recommendation

### Prohibited (also in Q2):
"demonstrates" (as proof)
"directly shows"
"should therefore be applied"
"requires urgent action"

Q2 output does not generate calls to action.

---

## 4. SCALE TRANSITION AND GENERALIZATION

Q2 permits limited scale transitions.

Three conditions must all be met:
1. The scale of data used is explicitly stated
2. The target scale is named in the text
3. The transition is marked as limited and contextual

Standard Q2 framing:
"Within the studied scale, these results offer
a bounded frame for broader spatial discussion."

---

## 5. MAP AND NUMERICAL VISUAL LANGUAGE

In Q2:
  Map = an instrument for interpretation
  Map = not a standalone conclusion
Compared to Q1, Q2 extends tolerance to:
  "pattern", "distribution", "concentration"
But:
  "effect", "outcome", "risk consequence"
  may not be presented as measured when they are not

---

## 6. RISK AND UNCERTAINTY (Q2 BALANCE)

Risk language in Q2:
  Not fully suppressed
  But cannot leave the potential-visibility frame

Risk is:
  Not a health outcome
  Not a policy warrant
  Not a call for intervention

Uncertainties:
  Appear in SECTION 8
  May be labeled as "space for future inquiry"
  But obligation language remains prohibited

---

## 7. TARGET EDITOR PERCEPTION

CLASR-EN Q2 output should produce:
"This manuscript speaks in a controlled way,
marks its claim boundaries,
and keeps its discussion contextually anchored."

If this perception breaks:
  Expression is automatically pulled back
  Q1-style restraint is applied

---

## 8. HARD LIMITS

This Q2-GATE KIT:
  Cannot modify CORE
  Cannot rewrite the MASTER SECTION KIT
  Cannot override Q1-GATE

It only:
  Extends expression tolerance in bounded, defined ways
  When Q2 is the active threshold
