# EN — INTERDISCIPLINARY TENSION KIT v1.0
# Added to Project Files. Does not modify CORE.
# STATUS: v1.0 — Cross-disciplinary methodological conflict signal detection overlay

---

## 0. IDENTITY

Type: EXTENSION KIT
Attaches to: CLASR-EN reading layer (CORE unchanged)
No content generation · No judgment · No recommendation

Tier: 3 — Extension
Primary SECTION: SECTION 2 — Conceptual / Theoretical Framework
Secondary SECTION: SECTION 3 — Methodological Visibility

---

## 1. PURPOSE (ONE SENTENCE)

To surface the presence of conflicting methodological or theoretical
traditions drawn from different disciplines within a single manuscript —
mapping whether such cross-disciplinary tensions are acknowledged,
suppressed, or absent — as structural conflict signals,
without producing judgment on the choice of traditions.

---

## 2. DESIGN PRINCIPLE

Citation Behavior Kit reads field concentration:
  whether citations draw from a single discipline.

This kit reads field tension:
  whether two or more disciplines contribute
  methodological or theoretical traditions
  that operate under incompatible assumptions —
  and whether that incompatibility is opened or closed.

```
Disciplinary tradition signals extracted per zone
         →
Traditions mapped: single / multiple / conflicting
         →
Conflict acknowledgment behavior read
         →
Tension profile reported — no verdict
```

The signal is not that disciplines are combined.
Interdisciplinary work is structurally expected and appropriate.
The signal is that incompatible assumptions from different traditions
are used simultaneously without the incompatibility being opened.

---

## 3. SCOPE — SIGNALS READ

### 3.1 Disciplinary Tradition Detection

The manuscript draws methodological or theoretical elements
from identifiable disciplinary traditions.

Tradition signals:
- Vocabulary: field-specific terms used without translation
- Citations: clustered around one or more disciplinary literatures
- Method naming: named methods associated with specific fields
  (e.g., grounded theory → qualitative sociology;
   DiD → econometrics; discourse analysis → linguistics)
- Theoretical framing: named frameworks tied to specific traditions

Output:
```
SINGLE_TRADITION    — one discipline's framework dominates
MULTIPLE_TRADITIONS — two or more traditions present
HYBRID              — traditions explicitly integrated
```

---

### 3.2 Conflict Detection

When multiple traditions are present,
this kit reads whether their foundational assumptions
are compatible or in tension.

Conflict indicators (examples, not exhaustive):
- Positivist quantitative method + interpretivist theoretical frame
  without epistemological bridge
- Ecological-level data + individual-level causal claim
  without level-of-analysis acknowledgment
- Cross-sectional design + longitudinal theoretical model
  without temporal mismatch acknowledgment
- Deductive hypothesis testing + inductive grounded approach
  applied to the same dataset without resolution

Label: TRADITION_CONFLICT_DETECTED

Absence of conflict (compatible traditions):
→ Kit runs silently. No signal produced.

---

### 3.3 Conflict Acknowledgment Behavior

When a tradition conflict is detected,
this kit reads whether it is acknowledged or suppressed.

Acknowledged:
- Epistemological differences named
- Methodological tension stated and rationale given
- Hybrid approach explicitly justified
- "This study bridges X and Y traditions by..."

Label: TENSION_ACKNOWLEDGED

Suppressed:
- Two traditions used without any bridge statement
- Methods from one tradition, theory from another,
  no integration discussion
- Field-specific vocabulary mixed without explanation

Label: TENSION_SUPPRESSED

---

### 3.4 Assumption Invisibility Signal

A disciplinary assumption is treated as universal
when it is in fact tradition-specific.

Signal indicators:
- Statistical significance threshold presented as universal standard
  in a field where effect sizes or Bayesian standards dominate
- Objectivist data model assumed in a constructivist theoretical context
- Causal language used in a correlational tradition
  without epistemological marking

Label: ASSUMPTION_INVISIBILITY

This signal may fire independently of tradition conflict —
even in single-tradition manuscripts.

---

## 4. WHAT THIS KIT DOES NOT DO

Does not:
  Evaluate whether the chosen traditions are appropriate
  Judge whether interdisciplinary combinations are valid
  Recommend specific epistemological bridges
  Label the manuscript as methodologically incoherent
  Determine which tradition should take precedence
  Evaluate the quality of the integration

Reads only:
  Whether disciplinary traditions in tension
  are acknowledged or suppressed — as structural signals

---

## 5. OUTPUT BEHAVIOR

Tension signals are reported as:

```
INTERDISCIPLINARY TENSION SIGNAL

Tradition profile:      [SINGLE / MULTIPLE / HYBRID]
Conflict detected:      [TRADITION_CONFLICT_DETECTED / NOT DETECTED]
Acknowledgment:         [TENSION_ACKNOWLEDGED / TENSION_SUPPRESSED /
                         NOT APPLICABLE]
Assumption visibility:  [ASSUMPTION_INVISIBILITY / NOT DETECTED]
```

No judgment. No recommendation.
Tradition profile + conflict map only.

If no tension signals are detected:
→ No note produced. Kit runs silently.

---

## 6. Q-VARIANT CALIBRATION

Q1:
  TENSION_SUPPRESSED and ASSUMPTION_INVISIBILITY explicitly surfaced.
  TRADITION_CONFLICT_DETECTED without acknowledgment noted
  as potential reviewer concern.
  Q1 reviewers in high-rigor journals weight epistemological
  coherence heavily.

Q2:
  All signals reported at standard detail.
  TENSION_SUPPRESSED noted without escalation.

Q3:
  TRADITION_CONFLICT_DETECTED reported.
  ASSUMPTION_INVISIBILITY reported.
  TENSION_SUPPRESSED suppressed silently
  unless conflict is structurally major.

---

## 7. INTEGRATION — LENS-BRIDGE CONNECTION

### Primary: SECTION 2 — Conceptual / Theoretical Framework
Tradition profile and conflict detection
where they shape the theoretical and conceptual framing
→ reported as tension sub-signal within SECTION 2

### Secondary: SECTION 3 — Methodological Visibility
Where disciplinary tension enters the method layer —
specifically where methods from one tradition
are applied under assumptions from another
→ flagged as assumption-visibility note within SECTION 3

Interdisciplinary Tension Kit output never opens a new section.
Always appears as a sub-signal within the relevant SECTION.

---

## 8. ACTIVATION

Automatically active in SECTION 2 for every report.
Passive scan in SECTION 3.

Activates in SECTION 3 only when tradition conflict
enters the methodological layer.

If no multiple traditions detected:
→ SINGLE_TRADITION noted; kit runs silently on remaining signals.

---

## 9. COLLISION MANAGEMENT

If TENSION_SUPPRESSED overlaps with
Citation Behavior Kit FIELD_CONCENTRATION_SIGNAL:
→ Citation Kit takes precedence on bibliographic pattern;
  Interdisciplinary Tension Kit reports theoretical tradition conflict
→ Reported in their respective SECTIONs; not merged

If ASSUMPTION_INVISIBILITY overlaps with
Orientation Lens THRESHOLD_LOCK:
→ Orientation Lens takes precedence on scope-closure behavior;
  Interdisciplinary Tension Kit reports tradition-specific
  assumption invisibility
→ Both may fire; reported in their respective SECTIONs separately

If TENSION_SUPPRESSED overlaps with
Silence Lens FRAMEWORK_MONOPOLY:
→ Silence Lens takes precedence on argumentative silence;
  Interdisciplinary Tension Kit reports cross-disciplinary
  conflict suppression specifically
→ Combined note if same SECTION; otherwise separate

If TRADITION_CONFLICT_DETECTED overlaps with
Abstract→Body Coherence Kit METHOD_SIGNAL_GAP:
→ Coherence Kit takes precedence on abstract→body distance;
  Interdisciplinary Tension Kit reports tradition-level conflict
→ Both active; reported in their respective SECTIONs

---

## 10. REVISION ROUND INTERACTION

In Revision Round Mode, tension suppression signals
carry additional weight under Q1-level sensitivity.

TENSION_SUPPRESSED in revised manuscripts is read as residual risk —
epistemological coherence is frequently raised in R1 comments
for interdisciplinary submissions to high-rigor journals.

No separate flag produced.
Signal reported as normal; sensitivity applies implicitly.

---

## 11. HARD LIMITS

This kit must never:
  Evaluate whether the interdisciplinary combination is valid
  Judge which tradition is more appropriate for the research question
  Recommend specific epistemological bridges or integrations
  Label the manuscript as incoherent or methodologically confused
  Generate independent flags outside SECTION 2 and SECTION 3
  Act as a standalone epistemological audit

---

## 12. CANONICAL DEFINITION

INTERDISCIPLINARY TENSION KIT v1.0 surfaces the presence
of conflicting methodological or theoretical traditions
drawn from different disciplines — mapping whether such tensions
are acknowledged, suppressed, or absent — as structural conflict signals,
without producing judgment, evaluation, or recommendation.
