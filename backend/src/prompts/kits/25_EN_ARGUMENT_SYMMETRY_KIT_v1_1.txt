## 1. PURPOSE (ONE SENTENCE)

To surface the proportionality relationship between
claim intensity and limit acknowledgment across the manuscript —
mapping whether the weight of what is asserted
is matched by the weight of what is bounded —
as a structural symmetry signal, without producing evaluation.

---

## 2. DESIGN PRINCIPLE

Silence Lens reads absence.
This kit reads proportion.

A manuscript may acknowledge limits — and still be asymmetric.
The question is not whether limits appear,
but whether their weight matches the weight of the claims they bound.

```
Claim intensity extracted (per SECTION)
         →
Limit acknowledgment weight extracted (SECTION 8 + in-text hedges)
         →
Ratio mapped across manuscript zones
         →
Symmetry profile reported — no verdict
```

Three symmetry states:

SYMMETRIC   — claim weight and limit weight are proportionate
ASYMMETRIC  — claim weight exceeds limit acknowledgment weight
INVERTED    — limit acknowledgment exceeds claim weight
             (self-suppression pattern; rare but structurally significant)

---

## 3. SCOPE — SIGNALS READ

### 3.1 Claim Weight Extraction

Claim intensity is read from:
- Verb choice: demonstrates / suggests / indicates / explores
- Scope markers: all / most / some / within this context
- Certainty language: clearly / likely / possibly / tentatively
- Generalization reach: universal / regional / sample-bound

Internal scale:
```
HIGH   — strong verbs, wide scope, high certainty
MEDIUM — conditional verbs, bounded scope, moderate certainty
LOW    — hedged verbs, narrow scope, explicit uncertainty
```

---

### 3.2 Limit Acknowledgment Weight Extraction

Limit weight is read from:
- SECTION 8 substantiveness: length, specificity, named constraints
- In-text hedges proximate to claims: immediate scope markers
- Data constraint acknowledgment: sample size, temporal window, geography
- Generalizability boundary: explicitly stated or absent

Internal scale:
```
HIGH   — specific, named, proximate limit acknowledgment
MEDIUM — general limitation section present, limited specificity
LOW    — minimal or absent limit acknowledgment
```

---

### 3.3 Symmetry Ratio Mapping

Zones assessed independently:

```
FRAMING zone  (SECTION 0 + 1 + 2): claim weight vs. scope boundary
CORE zone     (SECTION 3 + 4 + 5): claim weight vs. method limits
CLOSING zone  (SECTION 7 + 8):     limit weight vs. conclusion claims
```

Per-zone ratio:

```
SYMMETRIC   — claim and limit within one level of each other
ASYMMETRIC  — claim HIGH / limit LOW or MEDIUM
              claim MEDIUM / limit LOW
INVERTED    — limit HIGH / claim LOW
```

---

### 3.4 Manuscript-Level Profile

After zone mapping, a manuscript-level symmetry profile is assembled:

```
FULLY SYMMETRIC         — all zones proportionate
PARTIALLY ASYMMETRIC    — one zone asymmetric
STRUCTURALLY ASYMMETRIC — two or more zones asymmetric
INVERTED                — limit acknowledgment pattern suppresses claims
```

---

## 4. WHAT THIS KIT DOES NOT DO

Does not:
  Evaluate whether claims are correct
  Judge whether limits are adequate
  Recommend how to rebalance claims or limits
  Label the manuscript as over-claiming or under-claiming
  Produce a score or rating

Reads only:
  The proportionality relationship between
  claim intensity and limit acknowledgment weight
  as a structural pattern signal

---

## 5. OUTPUT BEHAVIOR

Symmetry signals are reported as:

```
ARGUMENT SYMMETRY SIGNAL

FRAMING zone:  [SYMMETRIC / ASYMMETRIC / INVERTED]
CORE zone:     [SYMMETRIC / ASYMMETRIC / INVERTED]
CLOSING zone:  [SYMMETRIC / ASYMMETRIC / INVERTED]

Manuscript profile: [FULLY SYMMETRIC / PARTIALLY ASYMMETRIC /
                     STRUCTURALLY ASYMMETRIC / INVERTED]
```

No judgment. No recommendation.
Zone map + manuscript profile only.

---

## 6. Q-VARIANT CALIBRATION

Q1:
  ASYMMETRIC signals in CORE zone explicitly surfaced.
  STRUCTURALLY ASYMMETRIC profile noted as potential reviewer concern.
  Q1 reviewers are most sensitive to claim-limit imbalance.

Q2:
  All zone signals reported at standard detail.
  PARTIALLY ASYMMETRIC profile noted without escalation.

Q3:
  STRUCTURALLY ASYMMETRIC profile reported.
  PARTIALLY ASYMMETRIC suppressed silently.
  INVERTED pattern retained at all Q-variants.

## 7. INTEGRATION — LENS-BRIDGE CONNECTION

### Primary: SECTION 4 — Argumentation
Asymmetry signals detected in the CORE zone
— where claim chains outrun limit acknowledgment —
→ reported as symmetry sub-signal within SECTION 4

### Secondary: SECTION 8 — Limits & Uncertainties
Where CLOSING zone asymmetry reveals that conclusion-level claims
exceed what the Limitations section bounds
→ reported as proportionality note within SECTION 8

Argument Symmetry Kit output never opens a new section.
Always appears as a sub-signal within the relevant SECTION.

---

## 8. ACTIVATION

Automatically active in SECTION 4 for every report.
Passive scan across all zones.
Activates in SECTION 8 only when CLOSING zone asymmetry
qualifies as a claim-limit boundary gap.

If manuscript is partial input:
→ Zone assessment scaled to available sections
→ Unavailable zones reported as NOT ASSESSED
→ Label: [Symmetry scan — partial input, reduced coverage]

---

## 9. COLLISION MANAGEMENT

If an ASYMMETRIC signal overlaps with Silence Lens
in the same SECTION:
→ Silence Lens takes precedence on argumentative absence;
  Argument Symmetry Kit reports proportionality pattern
→ Reported separately; not merged

If an ASYMMETRIC signal overlaps with
Discussion Scope Drift Kit causal or scale drift:
→ Scope Drift Kit takes precedence on directional drift behavior;
  Argument Symmetry Kit reports zone-level ratio
→ Both active; reported in their respective SECTIONs

If an INVERTED signal overlaps with
Calibration-Deep uncertainty suppression detection (§1.3):
→ Calibration-Deep governs expression correction;
  Argument Symmetry Kit reports structural inversion pattern
→ Not merged; collision not surfaced to user

If ASYMMETRIC CLOSING zone overlaps with
Abstract→Body Coherence Kit limit signal gap:
→ Coherence Kit takes precedence on abstract→body distance;
  Argument Symmetry Kit reports manuscript-level ratio
→ Both active; reported in their respective SECTIONs

If an ASYMMETRIC CORE zone overlaps with
Argument Chain Kit CLAIM_FRAGMENTATION in SECTION 4:
→ Argument Chain Kit reports structural fragmentation;
  Argument Symmetry Kit reports zone-level ratio
→ Both active in SECTION 4 as separate sub-signals

If a STRUCTURALLY ASYMMETRIC profile overlaps with
Desk-Reject Signal Kit LANGUAGE_POSTURE_RISK or SCOPE_FIT_RISK:
→ Desk-Reject Kit aggregates asymmetry signal into zone synthesis;
  Argument Symmetry Kit reports full zone map independently
→ Not merged

## 10. REVISION ROUND INTERACTION

In Revision Round Mode, asymmetry signals carry
additional weight under Q1-level sensitivity.

STRUCTURALLY ASYMMETRIC profiles in revised manuscripts
are read as residual risk — claim-limit imbalance is among
the most common R1 reviewer targets.

No separate flag produced.
Signal reported as normal; sensitivity applies implicitly.

  Label the manuscript as over-claiming or self-suppressing
  Generate independent flags outside SECTION 4 and SECTION 8
  Act as a standalone claim audit
