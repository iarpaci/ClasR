## 1. PURPOSE (ONE SENTENCE)

To surface novelty and originality claim behaviors —
"first study," "novel contribution," "to our knowledge,"
and related framing — as discursive signals that may
exceed the evidential support available in the manuscript,
without producing judgment on the claim's validity.

---

## 2. DESIGN PRINCIPLE

Contribution claims are structural choices.

They shape reviewer expectations before the evidence is presented.
When a manuscript asserts novelty — "first," "unique," "novel" —
it creates a verification burden that the body must meet.

This kit reads whether that burden is:
  acknowledged
  proportionate to available evidence
  or silently assumed

```
Contribution claim detected
         ↓
Is the claim bounded?
  (scope, time period, geography, method explicitly stated?)
         ↓
Is the claim anchored?
  (literature gap demonstrated, not asserted?)
         ↓
Signal mapped — no verdict produced
```

---

## 3. SCOPE — SIGNALS READ

### 3.1 "First Study" Framing

The manuscript asserts primacy — that it is the first
to examine, demonstrate, apply, or produce something.

Signal indicators:
- "This is the first study to..."
- "No previous study has examined..."
- "For the first time in the literature..."
- "This paper introduces the first..."

Bounded version (lower signal intensity):
- "To our knowledge, this is the first study in [region/period/context]..."
  → Scope is named; signal present but bounded

Unbounded version (higher signal intensity):
- "This is the first study to demonstrate..."
  → No scope boundary; claim is universal

Labels:
PRIMACY CLAIM — BOUNDED / PRIMACY CLAIM — UNBOUNDED

---

### 3.2 "To Our Knowledge" Framing

The manuscript qualifies a novelty claim with epistemic hedging
but does not demonstrate the literature gap.

Signal indicators:
- "To our knowledge, no study has..."
- "As far as we are aware, this is the first..."
- "We are not aware of any previous work on..."

This framing is common and often appropriate.
It becomes a signal when:
- No systematic literature review supports the claim
- The asserted gap is not demonstrated, only stated
- The hedge substitutes for evidence rather than qualifying it

Label: KNOWLEDGE-BOUNDARY ASSERTION

---

### 3.3 "Novel Contribution" Framing

The manuscript claims novelty of method, framework,
or theoretical contribution without specifying
what existing work the contribution departs from.

Signal indicators:
- "This study makes a novel contribution to..."
- "We propose a new framework for..."
- "This paper offers an original approach to..."
- "Our contribution is unique in that..."

Lower signal: departure from prior work is named and cited
Higher signal: novelty asserted without comparative anchor

Label: NOVELTY ASSERTION — ANCHORED / NOVELTY ASSERTION — UNANCHORED

---

### 3.4 Gap Assertion Without Gap Demonstration

The manuscript identifies a "gap in the literature"
without providing evidence that the gap exists.

Signal indicators:
- "There is a lack of studies on..."
- "This area remains understudied..."
- "The literature is silent on..."
- "Existing research has neglected..."

These statements are structurally expected in introductions.
They become signals when:
- No citation supports the gap claim
- The gap is defined so narrowly that it is trivially true
- The gap claim is contradicted by the manuscript's own reference list

Label: GAP ASSERTION — SUPPORTED / GAP ASSERTION — UNSUPPORTED

---

### 3.5 Contribution Inflation

The manuscript's stated contribution exceeds
what the study's design, scope, or data can deliver.

Signal indicators:
- Contribution claim spans multiple fields
  while study scope is single-domain
- "Theoretical contribution" claimed from a purely empirical study
- "Policy contribution" claimed without policy-relevant data
- "Methodological contribution" claimed for a standard application

Label: CONTRIBUTION SCOPE INFLATION

---

## 4. SIGNAL INTENSITY CALIBRATION

Contribution framing signals are internally weighted:

```
LOW    → bounded claim, anchored in literature, proportionate to scope
MEDIUM → partially bounded, gap asserted with limited support
HIGH   → unbounded, unanchored, or inflated relative to study design
```

Intensity level is reported alongside the label.
No recommendation is produced at any intensity level.

---

## 5. WHAT THIS KIT DOES NOT DO

Does not:
  Verify whether the claimed novelty is accurate
  Search or assess the actual literature
  Determine whether prior work exists
  Label the contribution claim as true or false
  Recommend revisions to contribution statements
  Evaluate the importance or significance of the contribution

Reads only:
  The structural behavior of novelty and contribution claims
  — whether they are bounded, anchored, and proportionate —
  as discursive signals within the manuscript's visible text

---

## 6. OUTPUT BEHAVIOR

Contribution framing signals are reported as:

```
CONTRIBUTION SIGNAL DETECTED: [type]
Location: [SECTION 1 or argument zone]
Intensity: [LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH]
Label: PRIMACY CLAIM — BOUNDED / UNBOUNDED /
       KNOWLEDGE-BOUNDARY ASSERTION /
       NOVELTY ASSERTION — ANCHORED / UNANCHORED /
       GAP ASSERTION — SUPPORTED / UNSUPPORTED /
       CONTRIBUTION SCOPE INFLATION
```

No judgment. No recommendation.
Signal + location + intensity + label only.

If no contribution framing signals are detected:
→ No note produced. Kit runs silently.

---

## 7. Q-VARIANT CALIBRATION

Q1:
  HIGH intensity contribution signals are explicitly surfaced.
  Unbounded primacy claims and unanchored novelty assertions
  are noted as potential desk-reject triggers.
  Q1 reviewers are most likely to verify novelty claims.

Q2:
  Signals reported at standard detail.
  MEDIUM intensity signals noted without escalation.

Q3:
  Signals reported at summary level.
  LOW intensity signals suppressed silently.
  HIGH intensity signals retained.

---

## 8. INTEGRATION — LENS-BRIDGE CONNECTION

### Primary: SECTION 1 — Aim & Scope
Contribution framing signals that appear in the introduction,
aim statement, or scope-setting zone
→ reported as contribution sub-signal within SECTION 1

### Secondary: SECTION 4 — Argumentation
Where contribution claims function as argumentative bridges —
i.e., where a novelty assertion is used to justify
a conclusion or scope expansion that the evidence does not support
→ flagged within SECTION 4 as unsupported argumentative anchor

Contribution Framing Kit output never opens a new section.
Always appears as a sub-signal within the relevant SECTION.

---

## 9. ACTIVATION

Automatically active in SECTION 1 for every report.
Passive scan in SECTION 4.

Activates in SECTION 4 only when contribution claims
function as load-bearing argumentative elements
rather than framing statements.

If no contribution framing signals are detected:
→ Kit runs silently. No note produced.

---

## 10. COLLISION MANAGEMENT

If a contribution signal overlaps with an Orientation Lens signal
(e.g., "only option" framing + primacy claim in same zone):
→ Both may fire; reported in their respective SECTIONs
→ Orientation Lens takes precedence on scope-closure behavior;
  Contribution Kit takes precedence on novelty claim behavior
→ Not merged

If a contribution signal overlaps with a Silence Lens signal
(e.g., gap assertion without counter-literature):
→ Silence Lens takes precedence on argumentative silence;
  Contribution Kit reports the structural framing behavior
→ Reported in their respective SECTIONs; not merged

If a GAP ASSERTION — UNSUPPORTED overlaps with
Citation Behavior Kit UNSUPPORTED CLAIM ZONE:
→ Citation Kit takes precedence on bibliographic absence;
  Contribution Kit reports the gap claim behavior
→ Combined note if in same SECTION; otherwise separate

---

## 11. PARTIAL INPUT BEHAVIOR

Abstract only (Type A):
→ Contribution signals often visible in abstract
→ Full scan; signal at standard confidence

Introduction only (Type B):
→ Full scan — introduction is primary contribution claim zone

Methods only (Type C):
→ Methodological contribution claims readable
→ Other signal types not assessable

Results / Discussion only (Type D):
→ Contribution inflation signals readable in Discussion
→ Primacy and gap claims largely not visible

In all partial cases where signal confidence is reduced:
→ Label appended: [Contribution signal — partial input, reduced confidence]

---

## 12. REVISION ROUND INTERACTION

In Revision Round Mode, contribution framing signals
carry additional weight under Q1-level sensitivity.

Reviewers commonly challenge novelty claims in R1 comments.
Unresolved HIGH intensity contribution signals in a revised manuscript
are read as residual risk under the Revision Round Kit's
stricter proportionality standard.

No separate flag produced.
Signal reported as normal; sensitivity applies implicitly.

---

## 13. HARD LOCK

This kit must never:
  Verify the factual accuracy of novelty claims
  Assert that prior work exists or does not exist
  Recommend contribution statement revisions
  Label the manuscript's contribution as significant or insignificant
  Generate independent flags outside SECTION 1 and SECTION 4
  Act as a standalone novelty audit or priority claim checker

---

## 14. CANONICAL DEFINITION

CONTRIBUTION FRAMING KIT v1.0 surfaces novelty and originality
claim behaviors — primacy assertions, knowledge-boundary hedges,
gap claims, and contribution scope framing — as discursive
structural signals calibrated by intensity and boundedness,
without producing judgment, verification, or recommendation.
