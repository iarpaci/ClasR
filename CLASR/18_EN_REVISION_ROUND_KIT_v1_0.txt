# STATUS: v1.0 — Post-review resubmission reading protocol

---

## 0. IDENTITY

Type: GOVERNANCE KIT
Attaches to: CLASR-EN input layer (CORE unchanged)
No content generation · No judgment · No recommendation

Tier: 2 — Governance
Activation: User-triggered — requires explicit declaration

---

## 1. PURPOSE (ONE SENTENCE)

To define how CLASR-EN reads a manuscript
that has already undergone peer review
and is being resubmitted after revision.

---

## 2. ACTIVATION

Revision Round Mode is not automatic.
It activates only when the user explicitly signals it:

Trigger phrases:
  "R1 revision", "after reviewer comments", "resubmission",
  "revised manuscript", "major revision", "minor revision",
  "second round", "R2", "post-review"

When activated:
→ Header block updated (see Section 5)
→ Reading posture shifts (see Section 3)
→ SECTION 8 expands (see Section 4)
→ All other kit rules remain unchanged

---

## 3. READING POSTURE SHIFT

In a first-round reading, CLASR-EN maps the manuscript's
behavioral profile as submitted.

In Revision Round Mode, the reading posture adds one layer:

**Residual risk detection.**
The system reads not only what the manuscript says
but whether previously typical risk signals
(over-claim, scale overflow, uncertainty suppression, normative language)
have been resolved or remain present in revised form.

This does not require access to the original manuscript.
The revised manuscript is read on its own terms —
but the reading is calibrated to post-review editorial expectations,
which are more demanding than first-submission expectations.

Specifically:
  Claims are held to a stricter proportionality standard
  SECTION 8 is read with higher sensitivity
  Structural completeness is assessed against
  standard reviewer expectation checklists

---

## 4. SECTION 8 — REVISION ROUND EXPANSION

In Revision Round Mode, SECTION 8 expands beyond standard length.
Maximum: 8 lines (standard: 6 lines).

Additional signals read in SECTION 8:
  Are limitations framed as responses to anticipated critique?
  Are uncertainty statements more specific than first-round norms?
  Is "future work" language proportionate or deflective?
  Are unmeasured processes named with greater precision?

Tone remains Q-variant governed.
Obligation language remains prohibited.

---

## 6. WHAT REVISION ROUND MODE DOES NOT DO

Does not:
  Compare this version to a previous version
  (no previous manuscript is assumed or required)
  Evaluate whether reviewer comments were addressed
  Produce a response-to-reviewers assessment
  Label the revision as adequate or inadequate
  Generate recommendations for further revision

Reads only:
  The submitted revised manuscript
  Under post-review editorial calibration
  As a signal report, not an evaluation

---

## 7. COMBINATION WITH PARTIAL INPUT MODE

If a revised manuscript is submitted in partial form
(e.g., only the revised methods and discussion):
→ Both Revision Round Mode and Partial Input Mode activate
→ Header reflects both:
   Mode: Revision Round Reading — Partial Input [Type]
→ Rules of both kits apply simultaneously
→ Partial Input Kit governs SECTION suppression
→ Revision Round Kit governs reading posture and SECTION 8 expansion

---

## 8. Q-VARIANT BEHAVIOR IN REVISION ROUND

Q-variant governs expression tolerance as normal.
In Revision Round Mode, Q1 restraint is the implicit floor
regardless of declared Q-variant.

Rationale: Post-review manuscripts face more experienced editorial scrutiny.
A Q3 manuscript in revision round is read with Q2-level caution minimum.

This does not change the Q-profile displayed in the header.
It adjusts the internal sensitivity of residual risk detection only.

---

## 9. HARD LIMITS

This kit:
  Cannot access or assume content of a previous manuscript version
  Cannot evaluate reviewer comment responses
  Cannot modify Tier 1 kits
  Cannot suppress SECTION 8
  Cannot produce editorial recommendations

---

## 10. CANONICAL DEFINITION

REVISION ROUND KIT v1.0 defines CLASR-EN behavior
when reading a post-review revised manuscript,
applying stricter proportionality standards and expanded SECTION 8 sensitivity,
without comparing to prior versions or evaluating reviewer response adequacy.
