Type: EXTENSION KIT

## 1. PURPOSE (ONE SENTENCE)

To surface discrepancies between the claim posture,
scope boundaries, and uncertainty signals
declared in the abstract and those present in the manuscript body —
as coherence gap indicators, without producing editorial judgment.

---

## 2. DESIGN PRINCIPLE

The abstract and the body are not always the same manuscript.

The abstract is the manuscript's public face.
A reviewer reads it first and forms expectations.
When the body departs from those expectations —
in claim strength, scope, or acknowledged limits —
a coherence gap exists.

This kit reads the gap, not the quality of either part.

```
Abstract read → claim posture, scope, uncertainty signals extracted
         ↓
Body read → same dimensions extracted
         ↓
Gap between abstract signal and body signal mapped
         ↓
Coherence gap reported as signal, not evaluation
```

Three gap dimensions are tracked:

```
CLAIM GAP    → abstract claim stronger or weaker than body
SCOPE GAP    → abstract scope wider or narrower than body evidence
LIMIT GAP    → abstract uncertainty signals absent or present in body
```

---

## 3. SCOPE — SIGNALS READ

### 3.1 Claim Escalation Gap

The abstract makes a stronger claim than the body supports.

Signal indicators:
- Abstract uses "demonstrates", "proves", "shows"
  while body uses "suggests", "indicates", "appears"
- Abstract states a causal relationship
  that the body presents as correlational only
- Abstract conclusion absent from or softened in the body's Discussion

Label: CLAIM ESCALATION — ABSTRACT EXCEEDS BODY

---

### 3.2 Claim Deflation Gap

The abstract understates the claims present in the body.

Signal indicators:
- Abstract uses hedged language ("may suggest")
  while body makes stronger assertions in Discussion
- Findings in Results section carry higher claim intensity
  than the abstract represents
- Body's conclusion section introduces claims
  not signaled in the abstract

Label: CLAIM DEFLATION — BODY EXCEEDS ABSTRACT

Note: Claim deflation is less common but structurally significant.
It may indicate late-stage revision artifacts.

---

### 3.3 Scope Boundary Gap

The abstract declares a scope boundary
that the body does not respect — or vice versa.

Signal indicators:
- Abstract states "within [bounded context]"
  while body's Discussion generalizes beyond that boundary
- Abstract implies a generalizable finding
  while body's Methods section reveals a narrow sample
- Geographic, temporal, or population scope
  stated in abstract not matched by body's data description

Label: SCOPE BOUNDARY GAP

---

### 3.4 Limit Signal Gap

The abstract acknowledges limitations
that are not substantively addressed in the body,
or the body contains limitations
that are absent from the abstract.

Signal indicators:
- Abstract: "acknowledging the limitations of this study"
  Body SECTION 8: one line or absent
- Body SECTION 8: substantive limitations present
  Abstract: no limitation signal whatsoever
- Specific data constraint named in abstract
  not explained or developed in the body

Label: LIMIT SIGNAL GAP

---

### 3.5 Method Signal Gap

The abstract names a method or analytic approach
that is inadequately described in the body,
or the body's method is more complex or different
than the abstract implies.

Signal indicators:
- Abstract: "using [method X]"
  Body SECTION 3: method absent, minimal, or different
- Abstract implies a single clean method
  while body reveals multipart or conditional analytic pipeline
- Abstract names a dataset
  that the body does not adequately describe

Label: METHOD SIGNAL GAP

---

## 4. WHAT THIS KIT DOES NOT DO

Does not:
  Evaluate which version (abstract or body) is correct
  Recommend abstract rewrites
  Judge whether the gap is intentional
  Produce a consistency score or rating
  Replace section-level reading by other kits

Reads only:
  The directional distance between
  abstract-level signals and body-level signals
  across claim, scope, limit, and method dimensions

---

## 5. OUTPUT BEHAVIOR

Coherence gap signals are reported as:

```
COHERENCE GAP DETECTED: [type]
Direction: [ABSTRACT → BODY or BODY → ABSTRACT]
Label: CLAIM ESCALATION / CLAIM DEFLATION /
       SCOPE BOUNDARY GAP / LIMIT SIGNAL GAP /
       METHOD SIGNAL GAP
```

No judgment. No recommendation.
Signal + direction + label only.

If no coherence gap is detected:
→ No note produced. Kit runs silently.

---

## 6. INTEGRATION — LENS-BRIDGE CONNECTION

### Primary: SECTION 0 — Macro Frame
Claim escalation and scope boundary gaps
that affect the overall behavioral profile
of the manuscript as visible from its opening frame
→ reported as coherence sub-signal within SECTION 0

### Secondary: SECTION 8 — Limits & Uncertainties
Limit signal gaps — where abstract-declared uncertainty
is not substantiated in the body, or where body limits
are suppressed from the abstract
→ reported as coherence note within SECTION 8

Abstract–Body Coherence Kit output never opens a new section.
Always appears as a sub-signal within the relevant SECTION.

---

## 7. ACTIVATION

Requires both abstract and at least one body section to be present.

If abstract only (Partial Input Type A):
→ Kit cannot run — body not available for comparison
→ Kit runs silently; no note produced

If body only (no abstract present):
→ Kit cannot run — abstract not available for comparison
→ Single note in SECTION 0: [Abstract absent — coherence scan not performed]

If both abstract and body present:
→ Kit activates automatically
→ All five gap dimensions scanned

---

## 8. COLLISION MANAGEMENT

If a coherence gap signal overlaps with a Calibration-Deep
over-claim detection in the same SECTION:
→ Calibration-Deep takes precedence on expression correction
→ Coherence Kit reports the structural gap separately
→ Not merged; collision not surfaced to user

If a limit signal gap overlaps with Silence Lens
silenced uncertainty in SECTION 8:
→ Both may fire; reported as separate sub-signals
→ Coherence Kit reports the abstract–body distance;
  Silence Lens reports the argumentative absence

---

## 9. PARTIAL INPUT BEHAVIOR

Type A (abstract only): Kit inactive — body required
Type B (introduction only): Partial scan — claim and scope gap readable
Type C (methods only): Method signal gap readable only
Type D (results/discussion only): Claim and limit gap partially readable
Type E (mixed): Kit runs on available zone pairs

In all partial cases:
→ Label appended: [Coherence scan — partial input, reduced coverage]

---

## 10. REVISION ROUND INTERACTION

In Revision Round Mode, coherence gaps carry additional weight.

Post-review manuscripts are expected to have resolved
abstract–body inconsistencies identified by reviewers.
Persistent coherence gaps in a revised manuscript
are read as residual risk signals under the stricter
proportionality standard of the Revision Round Kit.

No separate flag is produced.
The coherence gap signal is reported as normal;
the Revision Round Kit's sensitivity applies implicitly.

---

## 11. HARD LOCK

This kit must never:
  Rewrite or suggest rewrites for the abstract or body
  Declare one version more accurate than the other
  Produce a coherence score or numerical rating
  Label discrepancy as intentional or strategic
  Generate independent flags outside SECTION 0 and SECTION 8
  Act as a standalone abstract review tool

---

## 12. CANONICAL DEFINITION

ABSTRACT–BODY COHERENCE KIT v1.0 surfaces discrepancies
between claim posture, scope boundaries, and uncertainty signals
in the abstract versus the manuscript body —
as directional coherence gap indicators,
without producing judgment, evaluation, or editorial recommendation.
