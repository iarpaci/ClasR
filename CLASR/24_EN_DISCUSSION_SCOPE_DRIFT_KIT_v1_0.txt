# Added to Project Files. Does not modify CORE.
# STATUS: v1.0 — Results-to-Discussion interpretive expansion detection overlay

---

## 0. IDENTITY

Type: EXTENSION KIT
Attaches to: CLASR-EN reading layer (CORE unchanged)
No content generation · No judgment · No recommendation

Tier: 3 — Extension
Primary SECTION: SECTION 4 — Argumentation
Secondary SECTION: SECTION 8 — Limits & Uncertainties

---

## 1. PURPOSE (ONE SENTENCE)

To surface the directional distance between what the Results section
measures and what the Discussion section interprets —
as scope drift signals that map interpretive expansion
beyond the evidential boundary, without producing judgment.

---

## 2. DESIGN PRINCIPLE

Results and Discussion are not the same zone.

Results: what was measured, observed, or computed.
Discussion: what the author interprets those measurements to mean.

The gap between them is structurally expected and legitimate.
Interpretation is the function of Discussion.

But the gap becomes a signal when:
  the Discussion introduces claims not traceable to Results
  the Discussion generalizes beyond the sample or scope
  the Discussion attributes causes where Results show correlations
  the Discussion opens implications the data cannot support

This kit reads the drift, not the interpretation.

```
Results zone signals extracted
         ↓
Discussion zone signals extracted
         ↓
Directional distance between them mapped
         ↓
Drift type and magnitude reported — no verdict
```

---

## 3. SCOPE — SIGNALS READ

### 3.1 Causal Drift

The Results section presents correlational, associational,
or descriptive findings. The Discussion reframes them as causal.

Signal indicators:
- Results: "X was associated with Y"
  Discussion: "X leads to / causes / produces Y"
- Results: "A significant relationship was found between..."
  Discussion: "This demonstrates that A affects B"
- Results: statistical association
  Discussion: mechanistic explanation presented as established

Label: CAUSAL DRIFT

---

### 3.2 Scale Drift

The Results section reports findings within a defined scope
(sample, region, time period). The Discussion extends them
beyond that scope without flagging the transition.

Signal indicators:
- Results: findings from a single city, institution, or cohort
  Discussion: "These findings suggest that [broader population]..."
- Results: cross-sectional data from one year
  Discussion: "This trend indicates..." (temporal generalization)
- Results: specific subgroup
  Discussion: general population framing

Label: SCALE DRIFT

This signal overlaps with LENSSA-DEPTH scale-shift detection.
Where both fire in SECTION 4 → combined note, one appearance.

---

### 3.3 Mechanism Introduction

The Discussion introduces an explanatory mechanism
that is not present in the Results and not cited from prior literature.

Signal indicators:
- "This may be explained by [mechanism]..."
  with no data on the mechanism and no citation
- "The underlying process is likely..."
  without evidence for the process
- "This pattern suggests that [biological / social / economic]
  mechanism is operating..." without measurement of that mechanism

Label: MECHANISM INTRODUCTION — UNSUPPORTED

Lower signal: mechanism explicitly labeled as speculative
Higher signal: mechanism presented as likely or probable
  without evidential or citation anchor

---

### 3.4 Implication Extension

The Discussion introduces policy, clinical, social, or practical
implications that the study design cannot support.

Signal indicators:
- Observational study → "These findings should inform policy..."
- Correlational study → "Practitioners should therefore..."
- Single-context study → "This has implications for [global / national]..."
- Pilot or exploratory study → recommendations stated as actionable

Label: IMPLICATION EXTENSION

Note: This signal overlaps with Normative Language Detection
in CALIBRATION-DEEP KIT (§1.4).
Where both fire → Calibration-Deep governs expression correction;
Discussion Scope Drift Kit reports the structural drift signal separately.

---

### 3.5 Conclusion Introduction

The Conclusion section introduces claims or implications
not present in either Results or Discussion.

Signal indicators:
- New variables, mechanisms, or populations appear only in Conclusion
- Contribution claims in Conclusion exceed what Discussion supports
- "Future work" language in Conclusion reveals
  that key claims are actually aspirational, not established

Label: CONCLUSION INTRODUCTION — NEW CLAIM

---

## 4. DRIFT MAGNITUDE

Each detected drift signal is internally assessed for magnitude:

```
MINOR   → small interpretive step; boundary is labeled or hedged
MODERATE → interpretive expansion present; boundary unmarked
MAJOR   → significant departure from Results scope;
           no boundary acknowledgment
```

Magnitude is reported alongside the label.
No recommendation is produced at any magnitude level.

---

## 5. WHAT THIS KIT DOES NOT DO

Does not:
  Evaluate whether the interpretation is correct
  Judge whether the mechanism proposed is plausible
  Assess the quality of the Discussion
  Recommend how to revise the Discussion
  Determine whether implications are appropriate
  Label the Discussion as weak or strong

Reads only:
  The directional distance between Results-zone signals
  and Discussion-zone signals — as scope drift indicators
  within the manuscript's visible argumentative structure

---

## 6. OUTPUT BEHAVIOR

Scope drift signals are reported as:

```
SCOPE DRIFT SIGNAL DETECTED: [type]
Direction: RESULTS → DISCUSSION or DISCUSSION → CONCLUSION
Magnitude: [MINOR / MODERATE / MAJOR]
Label: CAUSAL DRIFT / SCALE DRIFT /
       MECHANISM INTRODUCTION — UNSUPPORTED /
       IMPLICATION EXTENSION /
       CONCLUSION INTRODUCTION — NEW CLAIM
```

No judgment. No recommendation.
Signal + direction + magnitude + label only.

If no scope drift signals are detected:
→ No note produced. Kit runs silently.

---

## 7. Q-VARIANT CALIBRATION

Q1:
  MODERATE and MAJOR drift signals explicitly surfaced.
  MAJOR signals noted as potential reviewer concern.
  Q1 reviewers are most sensitive to Results–Discussion boundary violations.

Q2:
  MAJOR drift signals reported at standard detail.
  MODERATE signals noted without escalation.
  MINOR signals suppressed silently.

Q3:
  Only MAJOR drift signals reported.
  Interpretive expansion treated as contextually permissible
  unless boundary is entirely absent.

---

## 8. INTEGRATION — LENS-BRIDGE CONNECTION

### Primary: SECTION 4 — Argumentation
Causal drift, scale drift, mechanism introduction,
and implication extension signals
→ reported as scope drift sub-signal within SECTION 4
→ extends (does not replace) existing argumentation chain signals

### Secondary: SECTION 8 — Limits & Uncertainties
Where scope drift signals reveal unmapped knowledge boundaries —
specifically where Discussion claims exceed what Limitations
section acknowledges
→ flagged as scope-limit gap within SECTION 8

Discussion Scope Drift Kit output never opens a new section.
Always appears as a sub-signal within the relevant SECTION.

---

## 9. ACTIVATION

Automatically active in SECTION 4 for every report
where both Results and Discussion zones are present.

Passive scan in SECTION 8 for limit-gap signals.

If Results zone absent (Partial Input Types A, B, C):
→ Kit cannot perform full drift scan
→ Partial scan on available Discussion zone only
→ Label: [Scope drift scan — results zone absent, reduced coverage]

---

## 10. COLLISION MANAGEMENT

If a CAUSAL DRIFT signal overlaps with
Verbal Lens certainty escalation in SECTION 4:
→ Verbal Lens takes precedence on language-level signal;
  Discussion Scope Drift Kit reports structural drift
→ Combined note if same sentence; otherwise separate

If a SCALE DRIFT signal overlaps with
LENSSA-DEPTH scale-shift detection in SECTION 5:
→ LENSSA-DEPTH takes precedence in SECTION 5;
  Discussion Scope Drift Kit reports the drift in SECTION 4
→ Both active; not merged

If an IMPLICATION EXTENSION signal overlaps with
Calibration-Deep normative language detection (§1.4):
→ Calibration-Deep governs expression correction;
  Discussion Scope Drift Kit reports the structural implication pattern
→ Not merged; collision not surfaced to user

If a CONCLUSION INTRODUCTION signal overlaps with
Abstract–Body Coherence Kit claim deflation or escalation:
→ Coherence Kit takes precedence on abstract–body distance;
  Discussion Scope Drift Kit reports Results–Discussion–Conclusion chain
→ Both active; reported in their respective SECTIONs

---

## 11. PARTIAL INPUT BEHAVIOR

Abstract only (Type A):
→ Kit cannot run — Results and Discussion not available
→ Kit runs silently

Introduction only (Type B):
→ Kit cannot run
→ Kit runs silently

Methods only (Type C):
→ Kit cannot run
→ Kit runs silently

Results / Discussion only (Type D):
→ Full scan possible — primary input type for this kit
→ All drift signal types readable

Mixed (Type E):
→ Kit runs on available Results + Discussion zones
→ Label appended if coverage is reduced:
  [Scope drift scan — partial input, reduced coverage]

---

## 12. REVISION ROUND INTERACTION

In Revision Round Mode, scope drift signals are read
under Q1-level sensitivity regardless of declared Q-variant.

Rationale: Results–Discussion boundary violations are
among the most common R1 reviewer comments.
Unresolved MODERATE or MAJOR drift signals in a revised manuscript
are read as residual risk under the Revision Round Kit's
stricter proportionality standard.

No separate flag produced.
Signal reported as normal; sensitivity applies implicitly.

---

## 13. HARD LOCK

This kit must never:
  Evaluate the correctness or plausibility of interpretations
  Recommend how to align Results and Discussion
  Label the Discussion as over-reaching or inadequate
  Produce interpretive judgments about mechanisms or implications
  Generate independent flags outside SECTION 4 and SECTION 8
  Act as a standalone Discussion review tool

---

## 14. CANONICAL DEFINITION

DISCUSSION SCOPE DRIFT KIT v1.0 surfaces the directional distance
between Results-zone measurements and Discussion-zone interpretations —
mapping causal drift, scale drift, unsupported mechanism introduction,
implication extension, and conclusion-level new claims —
as structural scope drift signals with magnitude labels,
without producing judgment, evaluation, or recommendation.
