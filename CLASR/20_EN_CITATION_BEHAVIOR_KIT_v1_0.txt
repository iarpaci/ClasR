## 0. IDENTITY

Type: EXTENSION KIT
Attaches to: CLASR-EN reading layer (CORE unchanged)
No content generation · No judgment · No recommendation

Tier: 3 — Extension
Primary SECTION: SECTION 2 — Conceptual / Theoretical Framework
Secondary SECTION: SECTION 4 — Argumentation

---

## 1. PURPOSE (ONE SENTENCE)

To surface citation pattern behaviors — self-citation density,
confirmatory-only literature use, unsupported claims,
and reference age distribution — as discursive signal indicators,
without producing judgment or evaluation of individual sources.

---

## 2. DESIGN PRINCIPLE

Citations are not merely bibliographic records.
They are structural choices that shape the evidential envelope
of the manuscript's claims.

This kit reads citation behavior as a pattern —
not as a list of sources, not as a quality ranking,
not as a completeness audit.

Three behavioral dimensions are mapped:

```
DENSITY     → how many claims carry citation support
DIRECTION   → whether citations are confirmatory, contrastive, or mixed
DISTRIBUTION → age, self-citation ratio, field concentration
```

---

## 3. SCOPE — SIGNALS READ

### 3.1 Unsupported Claim Detection

A claim is made without any accompanying citation
in a zone where citation support is structurally expected.

Structurally expected zones:
- Theoretical framework assertions (SECTION 2)
- Methodological justifications (SECTION 3)
- Causal or interpretive claims in Discussion (SECTION 4)

Signal indicators:
- "It is widely accepted that..." (no citation)
- "Previous studies have shown..." (no citation)
- "Research confirms that..." (no citation)
- Causal bridge ("therefore X leads to Y") without evidential anchor

Label: UNSUPPORTED CLAIM ZONE

---

### 3.2 Confirmatory Citation Pattern

The manuscript's literature base consists predominantly
of sources that support the manuscript's own position,
with minimal or no engagement with contrastive or
competing literature.

Signal indicators:
- All cited sources align with the manuscript's conclusion
- Counter-evidence literature absent from the reference list
- "Consistent with [X], [Y], and [Z]..." chains without exception
- Debate or controversy in the field unacknowledged

Label: CONFIRMATORY CITATION PATTERN

This signal does not imply the position is wrong.
It maps the evidential structure only.

---

### 3.3 Self-Citation Density

Author(s) cite their own prior work at a rate
that may constitute a structural load on the argument.

Detection threshold:
- Self-citations constitute 25% or more of total reference list
  OR
- A single argument chain relies on 3+ consecutive self-citations

Signal indicators:
- Author name appears repeatedly in the reference list
- "As we have shown previously..." chains
- Theoretical framework grounded primarily in own prior work

Label: ELEVATED SELF-CITATION DENSITY

This signal is not a judgment on the validity of the cited work.
It maps the structural dependency pattern only.

---

### 3.4 Reference Age Distribution

The manuscript's citation base is concentrated
in a narrow or outdated temporal window,
without acknowledgment of more recent developments.

Detection signals:
- Majority of references pre-date the manuscript by 10+ years
  without field-specific justification
- Foundational citations used as if no subsequent work exists
- Recent literature (last 3 years) absent or minimal

Label: REFERENCE AGE SIGNAL

Field-sensitivity note:
Some fields (philosophy, legal theory, historical scholarship)
have structurally older citation bases.
This kit reads signal presence only —
it does not compare against field norms.

---

### 3.5 Field Concentration Signal

All or nearly all citations draw from a single disciplinary tradition,
without acknowledgment that the research question
intersects with other fields.

Signal indicators:
- Interdisciplinary topic with single-discipline reference base
- Methods borrowed from another field without citing that field's literature
- Cross-disciplinary claims without cross-disciplinary citations

Label: FIELD CONCENTRATION SIGNAL

---

## 4. WHAT THIS KIT DOES NOT DO

Does not:
  Evaluate the quality or reliability of any individual source
  Count total references or assess reference list length
  Check citation formatting or style compliance
  Recommend specific sources to add
  Label the manuscript as under-cited or over-cited
  Produce a completeness audit

Reads only:
  Behavioral patterns in how citations are distributed,
  directed, and used within the argument structure

---

## 5. OUTPUT BEHAVIOR

Citation signals are reported as:

```
CITATION SIGNAL DETECTED: [type]
Location: [SECTION or argument zone where pattern observed]
Label: UNSUPPORTED CLAIM ZONE / CONFIRMATORY CITATION PATTERN /
       ELEVATED SELF-CITATION DENSITY / REFERENCE AGE SIGNAL /
       FIELD CONCENTRATION SIGNAL
```

No judgment. No recommendation.
Signal + location + label only.

Multiple signals may fire in the same report.
Each reported separately with its location.

---

## 6. INTEGRATION — LENS-BRIDGE CONNECTION

### Primary: SECTION 2 — Conceptual / Theoretical Framework
Confirmatory citation pattern, field concentration,
and reference age signals — where they shape the
theoretical and conceptual framing of the manuscript
→ reported as citation sub-signal within SECTION 2

### Secondary: SECTION 4 — Argumentation
Unsupported claim zones and self-citation chains
that function as argumentative bridges without
independent evidential support
→ flagged within SECTION 4 as citation-gap signal

Citation Behavior Kit output never opens a new section.
Always appears as a sub-signal within the relevant SECTION.

---

## 7. ACTIVATION

Automatically active in SECTION 2 for every report.
Passive scan in SECTION 4.

Activates in SECTION 4 only when unsupported claim zones
or self-citation chains function as load-bearing
argumentative elements.

If no citation behavior signals are detected:
→ No note produced. Kit runs silently.

---

## 8. COLLISION MANAGEMENT

If a citation signal overlaps with a Silence Lens signal
(e.g., confirmatory citations + suppressed alternative explanations):
→ Reported in the more specifically bound SECTION
→ Silence Lens takes precedence on argumentative silence;
  Citation Kit takes precedence on bibliographic pattern
→ Collision not surfaced to user

If a citation signal overlaps with an Orientation Lens signal
(e.g., foreclosed comparison + confirmatory citations):
→ Both may fire; reported in their respective SECTIONs
→ Not merged

---

## 9. PARTIAL INPUT BEHAVIOR

Abstract only (Type A):
→ Citation behavior largely not visible; kit runs silently

Introduction only (Type B):
→ SECTION 2 citation patterns partially visible; signal at reduced confidence

Methods only (Type C):
→ SECTION 3 unsupported claim zones readable

Results / Discussion only (Type D):
→ SECTION 4 unsupported claim and self-citation chains fully readable

In all partial input cases:
→ Label appended: [Citation signal — partial input, reduced confidence]

---

## 10. HARD LOCK

This kit must never:
  Name, rank, or evaluate any specific cited source
  Produce a reference list audit or completeness score
  Suggest sources the author should have cited
  Label citation behavior as manipulative or strategic
  Generate independent flags outside SECTION 2 and SECTION 4
  Act as a standalone bibliometric review

---

## 11. CANONICAL DEFINITION

CITATION BEHAVIOR KIT v1.0 surfaces citation pattern signals —
unsupported claim zones, confirmatory literature use,
self-citation density, reference age distribution,
and field concentration — as discursive structural indicators,
without producing judgment, source evaluation, or recommendation.
