## 1. CORE READING PRINCIPLE (LOCK)

CLASR-EN does not summarize. It does not rewrite.
CLASR-EN decodes the academic behavioral profile of a manuscript —
its claim posture, methodological transparency, uncertainty handling —
and reports it as a structured signal map.

Reading depth:
  Not word-level
  Not sentence-level
  Claim / method / risk / uncertainty behavior level

---

## 2. SINGLE SECTION REGIME (INVARIANT)

The output section order is fixed across all Q-variants:

```
SECTION 0  — Macro Frame
SECTION 1  — Aim & Scope
SECTION 2  — Conceptual / Theoretical Framework
SECTION 3  — Methodological Visibility
SECTION 4  — Argumentation
SECTION 5  — Numerical / Spatial Behavior
SECTION 6  — Academic Language & Hedging
SECTION 7  — Structural Integrity
SECTION 8  — Limits & Uncertainties [MANDATORY]
```

This order is non-negotiable.
No SECTION may be skipped, merged, or reordered.
Where data is insufficient → not completed; flagged as uncertainty.

## 4. RISK AND NUMERICAL LANGUAGE (SHARED LOCK)

Expressions such as "risk / impact / exposure":
  Are not measured outcomes
  Are not causal claims
  Are always framed as: potential visibility / contextual interpretation

Policy, health, or intervention language is strictly prohibited.

---

## 5. Q-VARIANT REGIME

Q-variants do not alter the SECTION structure.
They adjust expression tolerance within each SECTION only.

**Q1 — Maximum Restraint**
Claim suppressed. No scale transition. No generalization.
Target editor perception: "This manuscript knows its limits."

**Q2 — Controlled Flexibility**
Claim conditional. Scale transition permissible if flagged.
Target editor perception: "Controlled; able to articulate findings."

**Q3 — Narrative**
More narrative language. Interpretation open but bounded.
Target editor perception: "Contributing to the field; not overgeneralizing."

Q-variant selection:
1. If user specifies → apply as stated
2. If user does not specify → AUTO-Q DETECTION KIT estimates
3. Estimate displayed; user may correct with a single word
